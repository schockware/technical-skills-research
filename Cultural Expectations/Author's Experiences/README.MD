# Why this area exists at all

A framework that can call other people's expectations irrational has to survive being aimed at its own author. That is the entire job of this folder. It is the personal-grounding counterpart to [[__DISCLAIMERS_EXPECTATIONS_AND_BELIEFS]] (the fence and the danger) and [[___DISCLAIMERS]] (standing and limits). Before any axis gets to indict a CEO's expectation of a CTO, it has to indict mine — the expectations I carried into every team I led and consulted for, the ones that felt like description rather than demand. If an axis only ever fires outward, it is not a research instrument, it is a weapon with my fingerprints filed off.

So the experiences here are not evidence *for* the axes. They are the first place the axes get tested, and the place they are most likely to be wrong, because I have the most invested in not seeing it. Read them as the author submitting to his own apparatus first.

# The guard this folder carries: don't let a description become a justification

The biggest risk in personal grounding is that "here is how it has always worked, in my experience" quietly becomes "here is how it must work." A lived pattern is data about what *was*, not a verdict about what *should be*. The moment one of these anecdotes starts being used to argue that some arrangement is natural, necessary, or settled — that the status quo is the status quo *because it is correct* — it has been turned into exactly the kind of weapon the other disclaimers forbid. Description is not permission. The fact that I observed something everywhere I worked makes it a strong prior to *test*, not a law to *enforce*.
