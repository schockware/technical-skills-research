# Coverage Tracker — Axes × Anecdotes

**Purpose:** Map which axes the lived anecdotes actually reach, and *how cleanly*. This is a **stress-test sweep, not a checklist.** The goal is not one tidy specimen per axis — it is an honest map of where the evidence is thick, thin, awkward, or absent.

**The discipline (per [[___DISCLAIMERS]] and the collaboration rules):** when reaching for a specimen of Axis N, do **not** ask "what memory confirms this axis?" Ask "what do I actually find when I reach for it?" Three outcomes, all valuable:

- ✅ **Clean hit** — the axis has lived grounding. (Confirms what we expected; least interesting.)
- 🟨 **Awkward fit** — the specimen forces the axis to bend. *The bend is data about where the axis is mis-drawn.*
- ⬜ **Empty** — enough dysfunction experience exists and still no specimen. *The most interesting result:* the axis is rare-in-practice, mis-specified, or real-but-invisible-to-a-practitioner — and which one matters.

**Sequencing:** negative (dysfunction) samples first, then positive ("effective bubble") samples as the **counter-test**. The positives exist to falsify the bleakest finding — see [[00_COVERAGE_TRACKER#The-counter-test]] below.

## The counter-test

The dysfunction anecdotes all say: **brakes live in individuals, never in the process.** The positive "effective bubble" samples exist to falsify this. The falsifiable question:

> When I built a bubble of highly effective teams, did I install a **process-level brake** — or was I personally the brake? If the bubble dissolved when I left, that *confirms* the bleak finding rather than refuting it.

Positive samples get diagnosed against this question specifically. A genuine process-brake that survived the author's departure is the one thing that would break [[project-brake-location-pattern]]. Until then, the pattern stands.
