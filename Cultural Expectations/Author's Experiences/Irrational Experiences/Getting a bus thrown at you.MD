# Preface

You'll immediately spot my cheekiness and disdain.

# What happened
A major project had been announced. Millions of dollars of grant money came in. The problem. Only a few leaders and teams were included on the planning and they were committing people that didn't exist or were not in the room.

When the project failed to meet it's deadline the first set of people not in the room had "buses thrown at them".

Not thrown in front of a bus. They had no idea, "you were supposed to be working on this." No one had emailed or contacted them in any way.

They did an analysis of the work. The contractors claimed it could be built in 6 months. The group that had just gotten pancaked, said would take 2-4 years if they got enough people that day.

Leadership, "You have by end of year to finish this."

## Diagnosis

**Cell: Expectation × Illogical — a new species of the floor.** The Friday report was a self-contradiction in *time* (you can't be in two activities at once). This is a self-contradiction in *reference*: **you cannot allocate a resource that has no referent.**

> *"committing people that didn't exist or were not in the room."*

The plan was illogical at the moment of signing, before a single hour of work — its inputs were empty sets. This is not *unreasonable* (the people aren't overloaded — there are no people). It is not *irrational* (no human limit is reached — you never get far enough to reach one). It is `assign(∅) → deliverable`: a commitment that points at nothing. Two distinct shapes of the Illogical floor are now on the board:

- **Time-contradiction** (Friday report): the constraints collide in the same interval.
- **Reference-contradiction** (here): the constraints name resources with no referent.

Both are *floor-level* — no amount of time, money, or effort rescues them — but they fail for different reasons, and the taxonomy should hold both.

**The financial ratification is the trap.** *Millions in grant money came in* against the phantom commitment. The illogic got **financially validated before it was structurally checked** — the one diagnostic that would have caught it ("do these committed people actually exist?") was never run, because *money arriving felt like the check passing*. This is an Axis-10 flavor at the front end: funding was mistaken for measurement. The number that should have fired (headcount = 0) was drowned out by the number that did (dollars > 0).

And the relay refusal is already visible at the bottom: the pancaked group runs the real estimate — **2–4 years** — and leadership answers **"end of year."** Same move as *"we'll make it, work harder"* ([[We are not going to make it]]): the measurement is produced and then simply overruled. The illogical commitment cannot be absorbed by any relay, so it is converted into a deadline and pushed down.

# It got worse
Then our group had a bus thrown at us. "You were supposed to be working on this!"

We immediately went to investigate, "This isn't our system. And hi. Who are you?"

"Well it should be. And I'm the one telling you what to do now."

2 days later.

Manager, "Um... we now own this system."

"What did the owners of the system say?"

"What do you mean?"

"The group that controls that system."

"Oh right let me check." *one phone call later*

"OK we need to have a meeting. They didn't know we were getting that system."

## Diagnosis

**Cell: an attempted move OFF the grid — laundering, escalated past where the Friday report could go.** "Throwing a bus" is the same Axis-9 demand→person relocation, but with a vicious new property that breaks even the laundering frame.

In the Friday report, an expectation *existed* and was relocated onto the person who couldn't meet it. Here, **the expectation is invented after the failure and pinned backward onto people who were never told it existed:**

> *"They had no idea. 'You were supposed to be working on this.' No one had emailed or contacted them in any way."*

The unit-of-analysis slide is no longer "demand → person who couldn't meet it." It is **"failure → person who was never asked."** There is no expectation on the grid to relocate *from* — the expectation did not exist until it was needed as a weapon. This is the realm-fence violation in its most naked form: the verdict doesn't migrate from the Expectation row onto a person; it is **manufactured directly onto the person**, with no expectation-row antecedent at all. The Preface names it exactly — *"those are ethical violations, you can't normally do this"* — the machinery has stopped even pretending to track who agreed to what.

**The brake — and the third instance of the pattern.** In the first two anecdotes a single individual quietly absorbed the cost to stop the harm ([[The Report Due Friday End of Day]]: manager trashes the email; [[We are not going to make it]]: I hold the report and my tongue). Here the brake is **inverted**: nobody absorbs anything, and the harm only stops being compounded when one person runs one diagnostic question —

> *"What did the owners of the system say?" / "The group that controls that system."*

That single check — *who actually owns this?* — instantly reveals the system is **unowned in the transfer**: the real owners *were never told*. The org ran no such check anywhere in the chain; a phone call exposed the whole void. So the brake-location pattern holds a third time, from a different job, but in negative: the brake is not someone eating the cost — it is **the absence of anyone running the basic check**, and a single individual running it is what exposes the structure. The competence to brake (**Axis 5 — Internal Brake**) still lived only in a person, never in the process (**Axis 4 — Individually Demanded Competence**).

# Cannons lined in every direction
At this point, every group at every level had cannons lined towards each other, towards their own leaders, and the news was reporting about the mismanagement.

Oh by the way. Leadership still thought it was only going to take 6 months after 18 months had elapsed.

## Diagnosis

**Cell: Belief × (defended against measurement), recursively distributed.** This is the capstone act, and it is Axis 10 — the Measurement Paradox — in its purest form yet.

**The recursion.** Every prior anecdote had *one* relay refusing the math. Here the refusal is **distributed across the whole org at once**: phantom commitment → bus thrown at group A → group A's correct 2–4 year estimate overruled → bus thrown at my group → discovery that the system is unowned → owners never told. Each relay, unable to absorb an illogical commitment, passes it to the next as **blame rather than as a corrected estimate.** "Cannons lined in every direction" is the literal end state of the relay dynamic ([[The Report Due Friday End of Day]], Axis 6): when culpability for a structurally-guaranteed failure can't be braked, it doesn't dissipate — it gets aimed sideways, at peers, at leaders, at anyone in range. The bus is now everyone's, fired in all directions simultaneously.

**The 6-month belief is the bleakest data point in the corpus.** *Eighteen months elapsed, and leadership still believed it was six months of work.* This is the same belief mechanism as [[We are not going to make it]] — measurement produced, measurement refused — but here the refusal has survived **three times the original estimate of contradicting evidence.** The belief is no longer responding to *any* number; eighteen months of disconfirmation is simply not admitted. This is Axis 10's strongest form: not a memory deficit (the data exists in abundance — the news is literally reporting it), but **akrasia** — the org has the measurement, is surrounded by the measurement, and acts against what it means anyway.

And note what retired the belief in the prior anecdote vs. here. There, attrition (an exhausted manager) eventually closed it. Here the belief is held by *leadership*, the layer most insulated from the work and therefore from the exhaustion — so the attrition brake can't reach it. **The brake fails precisely where the belief is most protected from cost.** The news reporting is the only external brake in sight (Axis 5, external) — and it is reporting, not correcting.
