# 2.1c — C-Suite Composite Synthesis: the apple / banana / tomato / potato map

**Purpose.** Put all four C-suite composite roles (CTO, CFO, CMO, COO) on ONE sheet so the
**incommensurabilities become visible** — where a finding is verified for one role but missing for
another, and where a shared word ("craft," "composite," "IC→EM") is actually measuring different
substances. This is a **synthesis of work already done** (the 7 CTO JDs + 9 comparator JDs + Findings
C1–C9 + three verified deep-research runs), NOT new research. Its job is to let the reader point and
say *"that's an apple, that's a potato"* — i.e., to STOP false-uniformity comparisons before they're made.

**Reading the tags (per cell):**
- ✅ = verified (named source / coded data)
- 🔶 = candidate / qualitative only (directional, not quantified)
- ⁉️ = GAP — no research found for this role/cell
- ⚠️ = present but NOT the same unit as the CTO's (commensurability warning)

**The discipline this map enforces:** a tidy uniform table would COMMIT the apples-to-potatoes error.
So gaps and not-same-unit cells are first-class content, displayed, never smoothed over.

## Where the apples / potatoes actually are (reading the grid)

**The four roles are NOT one commensurable class. The shared words hide four different substances:**

- **Row 2–3 is the deepest non-commensurability.** "Craft" is four different things with different
  physics: engineering **decays** (can't bank), accounting **compounds** (banks cleanly), marketing is
  **fashion-cyclic** (taste), ops is **context-specific**. Every cross-role claim that uses "craft" as
  one variable is an apple-to-potato move. The CTO/CFO contrast on Row 3 is the ONLY pair we've
  actually measured (C7); CMO/COO craft-decay is **unmeasured** (⁉️).

- **Row 9 is the starkest gap and your original prompt.** The **~50% IC→EM regression** is a
  **tech-only measurement.** There is NO equivalent rate for CFO/CMO/COO. So that number **cannot** be
  used to say anything comparative — it's an apple with no potato to sit beside. Any synthesis that
  implies "the C-suite suffers IC→EM regression" is over-reaching from one role's data.

- **Rows 7–8 are asymmetrically evidenced** — CFO is census-quantified (✅), CTO is qualitative (🔶),
  **COO is a total gap (⁉️)**, and COO is the role where the people-leadership question matters MOST.

- **Row 12 is the one place they ARE commensurable — and it's the place the CTO is NOT special.** The
  identity rupture is universal (⚠️ across all four). Where the roles genuinely share a unit, the CTO
  has no distinctiveness. Where the CTO IS distinctive (rows 3, 5, 7, 11), the roles do NOT share a unit
  — which is exactly why the distinctiveness was so hard to see: **it lives in the incommensurable
  dimensions, not the comparable one.**

## The single most important structural reading

**The CTO's distinctiveness and the apples/potatoes problem are the same phenomenon.** The CTO differs
from the others precisely on the dimensions where the roles are NOT commensurable (craft decays vs.
banks; present-from-founding vs. hired-in; craft-anchored identity vs. strategy-anchored). On the one
dimension where they ARE commensurable (the universal identity rupture, Row 12), the CTO is ordinary.
So "is the CTO special?" was unanswerable as posed because it assumed a common ruler — and the honest
answer is: **the CTO is special in the ways the four roles can't be put on one ruler, and ordinary in
the one way they can.**

## The gap ledger (⁉️ cells — what's missing, ranked by how much it matters)

1. **COO is the least-evidenced role across the board** (⁉️ on rows 3, 7, 8, 11). It's the control that
   *fired* on the composite (Row 1) but is otherwise nearly unstudied — and it's the most people-centric
   role, so its EM-rung and identity-coherence gaps are the most consequential.
2. **No IC→EM regression rate for any non-CTO role** (Row 9). The corpus's hardest single number is
   un-paired. Either find equivalents or never use the 50% comparatively.
3. **CMO and COO craft-decay unmeasured** (Row 3) — the CTO/CFO decay contrast (C7) is a 2-of-4 finding
   presented as if it characterizes the whole suite; it doesn't yet.
4. **CTO feeder-path census missing** (Row 7) — CTO entry is qualitative ("one step from code"); the CFO
   has a real census. Asymmetric proof.
5. **Identity-coherence (Row 11) tested only for CTO/CFO** — the unifying mechanism rests on a 2-of-4 base.

## What this map licenses (and forbids)

- **Licensed:** "On the dimensions where the four roles are commensurable (the identity rupture), the
  CTO is ordinary; the CTO's distinctiveness lives entirely in dimensions where the roles are NOT
  commensurable (craft-decay, founding-presence, craft-anchored identity)." Rows 3/5/7/11 vs 12.
- **Forbidden (apple-to-potato):** using the tech ~50% IC→EM regression (Row 9) as if it were a C-suite
  fact; using "craft" (Row 2) as one variable across roles; treating the CTO/CFO craft-decay contrast
  (Row 3) as characterizing the whole suite; any COO claim beyond Row 1 (composite) and Row 6 (mild).
