## §0 — The thesis in one paragraph

The not-reasonableness around the CTO role is real, but it lives in a **legibility/attribution gap,
not in the role's design**. The role is **stage-contingent** — its required mix of hands-on, people-
leading, and strategic work is set by the company's stage — yet the artifacts that hire for it apply
the standard **context-free**, supply no scope boundary, and omit the trade-off that practitioners
openly name in their *commentary about* the role. When the composite then fails, the failure is
**attributed to the individual** rather than to the context-free application of a context-dependent
standard. (Moderate confidence; content-site evidence base.) The broader cultural line — why "smart
people can learn anything" is granted to software but withheld from medicine, law, and accounting —
is **not** explained by any single master variable. It tracks **whether a field installed external
legibility-and-accountability structure**, and that structure installs through **multiple co-equal
pathways** (named failure, complexity growth, market competition, jurisdictional competition,
academic restructuring), none necessary and none master. Software has reached internal self-
governance but fired none of the external pathways. The "smart generalist" prior is old (Fayol/Weber
at origin), and the recurring mechanism is the **title as a compression artifact** — a capability-
signal credential ("software engineer") that carries no scope boundary.

> **What this thesis deliberately does NOT claim** (each killed in R1/R2): it does **not** claim the
> CTO is unique among the C-suite; does **not** claim the three modes are intrinsically un-co-holdable
> (that is role design, and it was retired); does **not** crown a master variable; and does **not**
> have a verified intervention lever — only a verified *diagnostic*.

## §1 — T2: The legibility gap is in the artifact, not the role *(Core claim 1)*

**Claim:** For the CTO role, the cultural standard is applied **context-free** to a role that is
**stage-contingent**; the hiring artifact **compresses out** the trade-off that practitioner
commentary openly names; and failure is **attributed to the individual** rather than to the
context-free standard. The not-reasonableness lives in this attribution/legibility gap.

| Sub-claim | Status | Confidence | Source class |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1a.** The role is stage-contingent (required mode-mix varies by stage). | Documented | Moderate | Named practitioner (Fournier; SPMB archetypes) + content site (startups.com bands, "illustrative, not empirically derived") |
| **1b.** The JD presents the modes as one undifferentiated set, no scope-limiting language, no named trade-off. | Documented *for the sampled artifacts* (n≈2) | Low-to-moderate | Content site / recruiter template / jobs platform |
| **1c.** The trade-off is named in *commentary about* the role but not in the artifact that *hires* for it. | Documented | Moderate (cleanest leg — a contrast between two artifact classes, not a universal negative) | Named practitioner (Fournier) + content site |

**The mechanism, stated cleanly:** the knowledge exists (commentary names the breakdown at ~20–50
engineers, names "the unhealthy path" of being both builder and executive) and **does not travel** to
the decision artifact (the JD). That is the legibility gap, observed in the literature itself — and it
attaches to the artifact and the judge, never to what the work requires.

**The structural move that makes T2 survivable (from R3 Part 0):** the surviving T2 drops the
assertion that the three modes are intrinsically un-co-holdable. That assertion was role-design with
the subject swapped (R1 Lead 1, fatal to the corpus's stated form). The permitted claim reduces to a
property of the **judge and the artifact** — "a context-dependent standard applied context-free, with
mis-attribution" — and is falsifiable: it fails if JDs/judges *do* localize to stage, or if commentary
does *not* name the trade-off the JD omits.

**What §1 cannot claim:**
- **Not** that scope-limiting language is *structurally* absent across the JD population. The
  universal-negative is not earned (n≈2; the disconfirming population — mature-company JDs that split
  CTO/CIO/Chief-Architect or delegate to VP Eng — was named in the corpus's own Open Questions and
  never sampled). Restate as: "absent in the small content-site sample examined; population status open."
- **Not** that any of this is CTO-*specific*. See **G-1**. Until verbatim CFO/CMO JDs at matched stage
  are run, §1 is a claim about **C-suite hiring artifacts generally**, not about the CTO.

## §2 — T2: Why the standard stays in place *(supporting mechanism)*

**Claim:** The standard persists through conformity (generation), obedience (enforcement), and
**IOED + dissonance** (resistance-to-correction). These are of **mixed** evidential status and must be
presented as such.

- **IOED (Illusion of Explanatory Depth) — documented, robust, peer-reviewed.** Rozenblit & Keil 2002;
  Alter et al. 2010; Fernbach et al. 2013. The decision-maker is *confidently wrong*, not *uncertainly
  ignorant*, about the role's mechanism. **This is the load-bearing, verified leg.**
- **Conformity / obedience (the JD as Asch-majority; evaluators as Milgram-obedient) — argued, NOT
  documented for this domain.** Asch, Milgram, Bond & Smith, Burger, Meeus & Raaijmakers are
  textbook-canonical but **unverified-in-corpus** this pass, and lab-to-hiring-room transfer is
  untested. Carry as **plausible mechanism, not established.**

> **Discipline flag:** §2 must not lead with the conformity/obedience material or rate it "robust."
> R1/R2 found the corpus's confidence ran *inverse* to its evidence here. The verified leg is IOED;
> the canonical lab paradigms are a hypothesis about transfer.

## §3 — T2: The reflection gap is a DIAGNOSTIC, not a lever *(Core claim 3)*

**Claim:** Demanding a *mechanism* explanation — "by what process does one person hold all three modes
seed→scale?" — reliably drops felt understanding (IOED). This **exposes** that the judge cannot explain
the standard they enforce, which *is* the applied-vs-endorsed gap T2 locates. It does **not** reliably
**moderate** the expectation.

- **Evidence:** Rozenblit & Keil; Alter; Fernbach; Crawford & Ruscio 2021 (3 failed preregistered
  moderation replications); Sloman & Vives 2022 (consequentialist-bounded); Zeveney & Marsh. **All
  peer-reviewed, including the disconfirmations.**
- **Confidence:** diagnostic = **high**; moderation/intervention = **not established**.
- **The flag that survives even on the diagnostic:** transfer to identity-laden *role* beliefs is
  itself untested (R1 Claim H). If the genius-composite is identity-held, the explanation may bounce
  off. The diagnostic still locates the gap; whether it can ever become an intervention is open.

> **The single most important correction the synthesis must absorb:** the framework **no longer has a
> verified intervention lever.** It has a robust diagnostic (this section) and a dead/hypothetical
> lever (the dissenter — **G-4**). The synthesis must not present an exit it does not have.

## §4 — T4: Brakes install by MULTIPLE co-equal pathways *(Core claim 2)*

**Claim:** The line the culture draws is **not capacity-drawn and not explained by any single master
variable.** It tracks **whether a field installed external legibility-and-accountability structure**,
installed by **multiple co-equal pathways — none necessary, none master.**

- **4a. The multi-pathway model.** Abbott (1988), *The System of Professions* — jurisdiction shifts via
  failure/scandal, complexity growth, technology change, jurisdictional competition; corroborated by the
  corpus's own case spread (medicine = knowledge + military incentive, *no* named-failure trigger;
  actuarial/CAS 1914 = market; psychiatry = territorial; engineering = cumulative named failures;
  accounting = named failure). **Confidence: high. Peer-reviewed monograph + peer-reviewed cases +
  named institutions (ABMS, FAA, SEC).** *This is the corpus's best-evidenced general claim and it
  replaces the dead master variable.*
- **4b. Liability is NOT master — one co-equal instrument among several.** Malpractice predated specialty
  boards 50–80 yrs without causing them; guild self-regulation predated tort standard-of-care ~250 yrs
  and tort *defers* to professional norms (Bolam 1957); aviation's ASRS runs on *immunity*; GARA
  *removed* liability; the EU CRA installs software brakes by *regulatory mandate*. Kolstad-Ulen-Johnson
  1990; Faure 2014. **Confidence: high. Peer-reviewed + primary regulation. This is a genuine
  falsified-then-corrected finding (a model exposure).**
- **4c. Software's tier position.** Software has reached internal self-governance (Tier 2) but **no
  pathway has installed external enforcement** (Tiers 3–4); the ACM/IEEE professionalization attempt
  failed; the live *non-failure* pathway is absorption via adjacent licensed professions (DO-178C,
  IEC 62443, ISO 26262). **Confidence: moderate. Named institution + content site.**

**What §4 cannot claim:** no single trigger is necessary, and **"visible attributable harm" is NOT the
master variable** (**G-2**). If it appears at all, it is **one of several** co-equal triggers — symmetric
with the liability demotion.

## §5 — T4: The "smart generalist" prior is old, and the title is a compression artifact *(Core claim 4)*

**Claim:** The generalist-executive prior has a documented **pre-software theoretical origin**, and the
historical reclassification of programming shows the **title-as-compression-artifact** mechanism — the
same legibility failure as §1, with a pre-software instance.

- **5a. Origin (documented, peer-reviewed).** Fayol (via Bedeian & Wren; Hatchuel & Segrestin 2019):
  administration is general, teachable, and dominates technical specialty *at the top of the hierarchy*.
  Weber's office-not-person bureaucrat is an independent second channel. **Confidence: high at the origin
  nodes.**
- **5b. The title as compression artifact (documented, peer-reviewed + named academic press).** Ensmenger
  2010; Light 1999; Abbate 2012. Specialized work was performed, classified as clerical, and the
  reclassification machinery installed a **capability-signal credential** ("programmer" / "software
  engineer") that **carries no scope boundary** — importing engineering's authority without its
  liability, credentialing, or scope. *Does not require women having been numerically dominant — Misa's
  challenge is conceded.*

**Hard limits (the gap, stated in the synthesis, not buried):**
- The downstream transmission **Fayol → HBS/Chandler → modern CTO** is **unverified** (spend-limit killed
  the vote at 0-0; see **G-5**). "Descends from Fayol" holds **at origin only**; the path to the CTO is a
  **gap**, and may be Axis-2 convergent re-derivation, not lineage.
- Fayol's administrator is **post-technical by design**; the CTO composite is **technical-and-everything-
  else.** Fayol does not deliver the *technical-retention* element that makes the CTO distinctive. So
  Fayol is the ancestor of the **generalist-executive prior**, not of the CTO-specific composite.
- Surviving form: "The generalist-executive prior is old and has a documented origin (Fayol/Weber);
  whether the *modern CTO* inherited it or convergently re-derived it is open."

## §6 — T4: The cross-generational pattern is amnesia, not akrasia *(low confidence)*

**Claim:** The cross-generational software-failure pattern is **Axis 2 (amnesia — independent
rediscovery, broken citation trail)**, not **Axis 10 (akrasia — knowing-and-ignoring)**.

- **Evidence:** NATO 1968, Brooks 1975, CHAOS 1994 form no citation chain (CHAOS has no bibliography —
  the one firm node). **Source class: secondary** (primaries inaccessible). **Confidence: low** (demoted
  from the Axis summary's confident prose).
- **Limit:** citation is the wrong instrument for *practitioner* memory (transmitted via
  mentorship/training/ambient culture without citation). The reframe is **two inferential steps from the
  question** — carry as low-confidence, not resolved.
- **The firmer companion:** the *intra-generational* akrasia case is better-supported — Brooks/CHAOS are
  cited *within* the current generation (PMP/MBA curricula) and practice does not change. That narrower
  akrasia claim survives better than the cross-generational one.

> Do not present the 11-axis compound as a validated system with a causal ordering, and do **not** use a
> "civilizational akrasia" capstone (**G-7**). Use individual axes as **named lenses** where each has
> independent support.

## §8 — What the synthesis CANNOT yet claim (open gaps — disclose, don't bury)

1. **CTO-specificity** — needs verbatim CFO/CMO JDs at matched stage (G-1). Until then, T2 is about
   C-suite hiring artifacts generally.
2. **A real JD sample with a denominator** — including mature-company JDs that *do* bound scope — to
   convert §1b from "absent in n≈2" to a rate.
3. **Peer-reviewed contemporary sourcing** — the entire T2 spine is content-site; it carries descriptive
   weight (the JD *is* the artifact under study) but no inferential weight about CTOs-in-general.
4. **A negative control** (R2 4C) — a field with software's vulnerability profile that does *not* show
   the pathology — to make the profile predictive rather than fitted to the outcome.
5. **The Fayol downstream transmission** verified, or honestly re-labeled as convergent re-derivation.
6. **A non-extreme doctor thought-experiment** (R2 4B) — the rural GP / physician-CEO / surgeon-
   entrepreneur shows the medical brake is *partial*. State the anchor as "the brake in medicine is
   *stronger and more legible* than in software," not "the doctor composite is impossible."
7. **Whether the composite is held as a consequentialist or identity belief** — determines whether the
   reflection-gap diagnostic (§3) can ever become an intervention.
8. **The framework has no verified intervention lever** — only a verified diagnostic. This is a gap to
   state plainly, not to paper over with the dissenter hypothesis.
