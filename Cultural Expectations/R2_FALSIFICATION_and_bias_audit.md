## PART 2 — CONFIRMATION-BIAS AUDIT OF THE WORKFLOW

The corpus was built by an instance running deep-research workflows with an adversarial verifier. The verifier is real and sometimes kills claims (the "X-0" kill votes are evidence of genuine machinery). But the *machinery around the verifier* leaks confirmation bias in five specific, locatable ways.

### 2A — Leading research prompts that presuppose their answer. Severity: SERIOUS.
The "Argument being tested" headers are written as the thing to confirm, not as a neutral question. Examples:
- 2.1: *"CTO job descriptions encode an unreasonable composite demand."* The prompt already contains the verdict ("unreasonable composite"); the research then "confirms" it.
- 4.1b: *"The CTO's composite ancestor may be Fayol, not Taylor."* — the lineage is presupposed; the run looks for Fayol quotes that fit.
- 4.8: the entire section is framed as rescuing the loading claim ("the framework survives by switching mechanisms") — the survival of the claim is the goal, only the *mechanism* is negotiable.
The tell: when a disconfirmation lands, the section's response is overwhelmingly *"switch mechanism, keep conclusion"* (4.8 Price's Law → CCB; 3.9 liability → harm-visibility) rather than *"the conclusion may be wrong."* A workflow that always preserves the conclusion and only swaps the mechanism is doing confirmation work even when it kills individual claims.

### 2B — Cherry-picked source classes on the load-bearing contemporary claim. Severity: STRUCTURAL for T2.
The corpus has impeccable source discipline in the *historical* tracks (4.x: Bedeian & Wren, Abbott, Drucker primary, peer-reviewed law-and-economics) and **zero peer-reviewed sources on the contemporary T2 spine** (2.1, by its own admission). The part of the argument that is actually about the present-day CTO — the thing the whole exercise is *for* — rests on Wellfound, cto.academy, recruiter templates, and two WebFetch passes. This is not random thinness: the well-sourced parts are the historical color; the load-bearing present-tense claim is the worst-sourced. R1 cross-cutting #3 notes this; R2's method point is sharper: **the corpus is best-sourced where sourcing is cheap and historical, and worst-sourced where the claim must actually carry weight.** That is the signature of a workflow optimizing for citable-looking output rather than for testing the load-bearing claim.

### 2C — The "3-0 / X-0" notation launders confidence. Severity: SERIOUS.
"3-0 CONFIRMED" denotes an internal verifier vote, not three independent quality sources. A 3-0 on one Wellfound page (2.1) and a 3-0 on peer-reviewed Bedeian & Wren (4.1b) are typographically identical. The Axis summary then propagates the confident "CONFIRMED" language while shedding the source-class caveat lines. Downstream synthesis reading the Axis summary will systematically over-weight the content-site findings. R1 cross-cutting #1 flags this; R2 adds the **mechanism of contamination**: the notation is *uniform* across wildly different evidence grades, and the summary layer is where the caveats get stripped — so the bias compounds precisely as the material moves toward the synthesis.

### 2D — The three-mode taxonomy is assumed, then "found." Severity: STRUCTURAL.
R1 Lead 3 covers the circularity from the claim side. The *method* point: the Builder/Multiplier/Strategist frame is never sourced to an external validated competency model, never blind-double-coded, and has no inter-rater reliability. Binning any executive JD into three pre-chosen non-empty bins and reporting "all three bins are occupied" is a property of the coding scheme. The verifier validates *claims about the binned data*; it never validates *the bins themselves*. The adversarial machinery therefore cannot catch this class of error — it operates entirely downstream of the imposed frame. **The most consequential construct in the corpus is the one the verification process structurally cannot test.**

### 2E — Disconfirmations are "noted" then quarantined from the conclusion. Severity: SERIOUS.
Repeated pattern: a refuted sub-claim is recorded honestly (good), then walled off so it does not propagate to the headline. The cleanest instance is 3.9: the medicine/actuarial/psychiatry cases that fired *without* visible harm are in the file, and the very next move coronates "visible attributable harm" as master variable as if those cases did not exist. The disconfirmation is *present in the file* and *absent from the conclusion*. This is the inverse of disconfirmation theater (Part 3): not a fake demotion, but a real disconfirmation denied its consequence.

## PART 3 — THE DISCONFIRMATION-THEATER CHECK

Test: are the celebrated demotions (Price's Law, liability, Taylor) genuine self-correction, or does the corpus demote a *strawman* version and quietly keep a softened version of the same claim?

### Price's Law (4.8) — REAL demotion, but with a survivor that smuggles. Verdict: 80% genuine.
- **Genuine:** the √N form is killed in its home domain (Nicholls 7/48), Peterson named as contamination vector, the bibliometric law dropped. This is real.
- **The smuggle:** the *conclusion* ("composite lands on the most capable and breaks them") is preserved by re-founding on CCB. But 4.8's own Finding 5 (inverted-U, Xu 2021: highest contributors deplete *least*) **breaks the capability link entirely.** The rescue pivots to "involuntariness, not volume" — which can land on anyone and is *least* plausible for a high-agency CTO. So the demotion of Price's Law is real, but the surviving claim no longer supports the CTO thesis it was recruited for; the corpus reports the inverted-U honestly but does not metabolize that it has severed the load-bearing connection (R1 Claim J converges). **Demotion genuine; the survivor is a different claim wearing the original's clothes.**

### Liability (3.9) — REAL demotion, IMMEDIATELY re-offended. Verdict: the demotion is genuine; the lesson was not learned.
- **Genuine:** liability-as-master is decisively falsified across five domains with strong sources. Model exposure.
- **The re-offense:** the corpus enthrones "visible attributable harm" as the replacement master variable *in the same document*, with **zero direct tests** (admitted) and contradicted by ≥3 of its own brake cases and by Abbott's four-mechanism model. So this is not disconfirmation theater on the liability claim — that demotion is real — but it is the **exact over-reach the demotion was supposed to teach against, recommitted immediately.** The corpus demonstrates it can kill a master variable; it does not demonstrate it learned *not to need one.* This is the most serious method failure in the corpus because it shows the disconfirmation discipline is *local* (kills the named claim) but not *general* (does not update the disposition that produced the claim).

### Taylor "amplified the composite" (4.2) — REAL demotion, clean. Verdict: genuine.
- The layer/role split is a real correction (Taylor *decomposed* the supervisor into 8 foremen; rejected the composite generalist). The corpus does not keep a softened "Taylor sort-of amplified it" — it transfers the lineage to Fayol. This is the cleanest of the three.
- **The catch is downstream:** the Fayol lineage that *inherits* the load is itself only verified at the origin node (transmission unverified, spend-limit). So the demotion is clean but the replacement is under-built — and, as in 4.1b, the corpus does not apply its own broken-chain skepticism to it.

**Disconfirmation-theater verdict:** The demotions are **mostly genuine self-correction, not theater.** The corpus really does kill claims. The defect is subtler and worse than theater: **two of the three demotions immediately re-instantiate the same error class (a new master/loading variable) and protect the surviving conclusion by mechanism-swapping.** The self-correction is real at the claim level and absent at the disposition level. A corpus that learned its lesson would have responded to the liability demotion with "maybe there is no master variable" rather than "here is the next one, untested."

## PART 4 — SURVIVORSHIP / BASE-RATE BLIND SPOTS

The corpus samples failures and the institutions that formed around failures. It structurally never samples the populations whose existence would force revision. These are not oversights of execution; they are *designed-out* by the question framing.

### 4A — CTOs who DID hold all three modes successfully. Severity: STRUCTURAL.
The entire T2 frame requires the composite to be unholdable ("exceeds one person's reach," "no one can be excellent at all three simultaneously"). But the corpus never samples the base rate of CTOs who *did* hold Builder+Multiplier+Strategist across a stage transition successfully. They demonstrably exist (technical co-founders who scaled their own companies from seed to growth retaining architecture authority). Their existence does not need to be common to damage the claim — it needs to be *non-zero*, because "the expectation is unreasonable" becomes "the expectation has a low but non-zero success rate," which is a different and much weaker claim. **The corpus selects on the failure (post-mortems, reversion data, founder-separation accounts) and never builds the denominator.** This is textbook survivorship bias: studying crashed planes, never the ones that landed.

### 4B — Successful composite holders in the comparison professions. Severity: SERIOUS.
The doctor thought experiment (T4's anchor) asserts the doctor-composite is "instantly absurd." But the corpus never samples the real population of physicians who *do* hold composites — the rural GP who does obstetrics + minor surgery + anesthesia + practice management; the physician-CEO; the surgeon-inventor-entrepreneur. Their existence shows the line between "absurd composite" and "celebrated composite" is fuzzier than the thought experiment's bright line, and that the brake the corpus attributes to medicine is *partial*, not total. The thought experiment's "immediacy is the data" claim is itself an availability artifact (the *most extreme* list — surgery + CDL + rockets — is chosen, not a representative one).

### 4C — Fields that have software's vulnerability profile but DON'T show the pathology. Severity: SERIOUS.
The axis stack predicts software's brake-failure from a vulnerability profile (invisible complexity, no liability, wild-originated language, broken loop). A real test needs a field with the *same* profile that *doesn't* produce the composite pathology — a negative control. The corpus offers science as a "cousin" but treats it as *confirming* (same vulnerability, similar failure). It never seeks the disconfirming case: a field with the profile and no pathology. Without it, the profile is fitted to the outcome, not predictive of it. (R1 cross-cutting #4 notes the absence of negative controls; R2's base-rate framing makes explicit *which* population is missing and why its absence is fatal to a predictive reading.)

### 4D — JDs that DO bound scope. Severity: SERIOUS.
2.1's "scope-limiting language is structurally absent" is a universal-negative drawn from a tiny confirming sample. The falsifying population — mature-company CTO JDs that explicitly delegate to VP Eng, or specify a CTO/CIO/Chief Architect split as the de facto scope bound — is named in the corpus's own Open Questions (#3, #4) and never sampled. The corpus *knows* where the disconfirming population lives and does not go there.

**Base-rate verdict:** the corpus has no denominators anywhere. Every quantitative-feeling claim ("the composite is the default," "the role exceeds one person") is a numerator (failures, composite JDs) with no denominator (successes, scope-bounded JDs). This is the deepest method problem after the unfalsifiable separation — and it is *curable* (the denominators are samplable) but uncured.

## VERDICT — Is the method sound enough to support a synthesis?

**Partially. The synthesis can proceed only with explicit quarantine.** The corpus has a real adversarial verifier and a real disconfirmation discipline at the *claim* level — these are genuine and put it well above advocacy work. But the *method around* the verifier leaks confirmation bias in a consistent, non-random direction: **rigor is deployed where it demotes a claim the builder was willing to lose, and withheld where it would damage a claim the builder wants to keep.** R1 reached this conclusion from the claims; R2 confirms it from the workflow and adds the deeper diagnosis: the disconfirmation discipline is *local but not general* — it kills named claims but does not update the disposition (the hunger for a master variable, the preserved conclusion under mechanism-swaps, the imposed frame the verifier can't reach).

**Two claims are unfalsifiable as stated and must not anchor the synthesis:**
1. **The T2 expectation/role-design separation** (no operational distinguishing test exists; role-design impossibility is re-imported under an expectation label). Either operationalize the separating sentence or re-scope T2 to a pure legibility/attribution claim — which costs most of the axis stack.
2. **The compound (11 axes + ambient condition)** as a system — no stated falsifier; convergence of under-determined axes reads stronger than it is.

**QUARANTINE LIST (claims that must not be load-bearing in synthesis until repaired):**
- **CTO-specificity** — relabel from "under-powered" to "untested"; run the verbatim CFO/CMO comparison or drop CTO-uniqueness.
- **"Visible attributable harm" as master variable** — demote to one-of-several, symmetric with the liability demotion; stop minting master variables.
- **The Builder/Multiplier/Strategist frame** — mark as a heuristic, not a discovered structure, until externally validated and blind-double-coded; flag that every "composite confirmed" finding inherits its circularity.
- **The dissenter-as-robust-lever** — do not present as robust until the figures are verified-in-corpus and lab-to-hiring-room transfer is addressed; do not let confidence run inverse to evidence.
- **Fayol → CTO transmission** — present Fayol as origin only; flag the lineage as an unverified gap (or an Axis-2 convergent-rediscovery candidate).

**SURVIVES as sound (use freely):**
- The **liability demotion** (3.9) — model exposure and falsification.
- The **Price's-Law demotion** (4.8) — genuine; but cite "cumulative advantage," and do *not* re-attach the burnout story to capability (the inverted-U severed it).
- The **5.6 diagnostic/intervention split** — the best-disciplined section; just do not over-extend the diagnostic to identity-laden role beliefs without a flag.
- The **3.10 amnesia/akrasia split** — honest and useful, but label it low-confidence (wrong instrument for practitioner memory), not resolved.
- The **disconfirmation discipline itself** at the claim level — real, and the corpus's genuine strength.

**Bottom line for the orchestrator:** the corpus is sound enough to support a synthesis *of the historical pattern* (the 4.x tracks, well-sourced and honestly demoted) and *of the mechanism distinctions* (5.6, 3.10). It is **not** sound enough to support a synthesis that asserts CTO-uniqueness, a single master variable, the clean expectation/role-design separation as a finding, or the three-mode frame as a discovered structure. Those four must be carried as open questions or quarantined claims, not as load-bearing conclusions. The method's defining bias — asymmetric rigor that protects the keepers — means the synthesis must apply *extra* skepticism precisely to the claims the corpus states most confidently.
