## PART 0 — THE ONE STRUCTURAL MOVE THAT SAVES T2

R1 Lead 1 / R2 Falsification #1 are correct and fatal *to the corpus's stated form* of T2. The corpus claimed the irrationality sits in the expectation, but its evidence (three modes that "conflict and cannot be co-held") is a claim about role structure with the subject swapped. That cannot stand, and the steelman does not try to save it.

**What survives is a narrower, genuinely separable claim.** Drop the assertion that the modes are intrinsically un-co-holdable (that is role design, and it was retired). Keep only what is about the *judge and the artifact*:

> **The surviving T2 claim is about legibility and attribution, not about capacity.** The role is **stage-contingent** (its required mode-mix is set by stage/context — documented). The cultural artifacts that hire for it (JDs) and the judges who apply them **do not localize the role to its stage**: they present the modes as one undifferentiated standing set, supply no scope boundary, and never name the trade-off — while the people who *reflect on* the role (practitioners, in commentary) routinely do. The irrationality is therefore **not** "one person cannot do three jobs" (capacity / role design — forbidden). It is "the standard is applied **context-free** to a role everyone's own commentary admits is **context-dependent**, and the attribution of failure lands on the individual rather than on the context-free standard."

This is the contingency-theory reading R1 itself named as the cleaner position (R1 Lead 1, "What the corpus must do to answer"). It is strictly weaker than the corpus's framing and it is the only form that does not smuggle role design. Everything below is built on this re-scoped T2.

**Why this is genuinely separable (the operational test R2 #1 said was missing):**
- *Forbidden sentence (role design):* "The expectation is unreasonable because no one can be excellent at Builder, Multiplier, and Strategist simultaneously." (Reduces to what the work requires.)
- *Permitted sentence (attribution/legibility):* "The expectation is mis-applied because it is held context-free for a role the same population describes as context-dependent, and failure is attributed to the person rather than to the context-free application." (Reduces to a property of the judge and the artifact, testable without any claim about whether the composite is holdable.)

The permitted sentence is falsifiable: it fails if JDs/judges *do* localize to stage, or if commentary does *not* name the trade-off the JD omits. The corpus's own Findings 2 and 4 (below) are exactly that test, and they pass — for a content-site evidence base.

## PART 1 — T2: STRONGEST SURVIVING FORM

### T2 surviving thesis (re-scoped)

> For the CTO role, the cultural standard is applied **context-free** to a role that is **stage-contingent**, the hiring artifact **compresses out** the trade-off that practitioner commentary openly names, and failure is **attributed to the individual** rather than to the context-free standard. The irrationality lives in this attribution/legibility gap — not in the role's design.

### The load-bearing surviving claims, with honest confidence

**T2-1. The role is stage-contingent (required mode-mix varies by stage).**
- Status: **Documented.** Confidence: **moderate.**
- Evidence: SPMB's Architect→Scaler→Strategic-Operator archetypes; startups.com engineer-count bands (early "writes most of the code" → scale-up "rarely writes production code"); Camille Fournier (the operational and strategic roles are "each a full-time job"). (02 Findings 2, 5.)
- Source class: **named practitioner** (Fournier; SPMB exec-search marketing copy) + **content site** (startups.com; engineer-count bands "illustrative, not empirically derived" — admitted). No peer-reviewed source.
- Note: This is the corpus's *best-supported* leg and, per R1 Lead 1, it is the one that does the saving work — because stage-contingency is what makes context-free application a real error rather than a capacity claim.

**T2-2. The hiring artifact (JD) presents the modes as one undifferentiated set, with no scope-limiting language and no named trade-off.**
- Status: **Documented for the sampled artifacts.** Confidence: **low-to-moderate** (downgraded from the corpus's "medium" per R1 Claim B / R2 #2: negative finding by inspection of n≈2).
- Evidence: Wellfound JD (two WebFetch passes, "no such sentence exists"); cto.academy; corroborated across Indeed/Monster/LeadDev/CIO.com. (02 Findings 1, 3.)
- Source class: **content site / recruiter template / jobs platform**. Zero peer-reviewed (admitted). The "3-0" here is a verifier vote over content-site pages, not three quality sources.
- **What it cannot claim:** that scope-limiting language is *structurally* absent across the population. The universal-negative is not earned (R2 4D — the disconfirming population, mature-company JDs that delegate to VP Eng or specify CTO/CIO/Chief-Architect splits, was named in the corpus's own Open Questions and never sampled). Restate as: "absent in the small content-site sample examined; population status open."

**T2-3. The trade-off is named in commentary *about* the role but not in the artifact that *hires* for it.**
- Status: **Documented.** Confidence: **moderate** — this is the cleanest T2 finding because it is a *contrast* between two artifact classes, not a universal negative.
- Evidence: startups.com names "the unhealthy path: trying to be both hands-on builder and executive leader… leading to burnout"; Fournier names the breakdown at ~20–50 engineers; the JDs name none of it. (02 Finding 4.)
- Source class: **named practitioner** (Fournier) + **content site**.
- Why it is load-bearing: it *is* the legibility gap, in the literature itself. The knowledge exists (commentary) and does not travel to the decision artifact (JD). This is the surviving mechanism — and it attaches cleanly to the attribution/legibility locus, not to role design.

**T2-4. The mechanisms that keep the standard in place are conformity (generation), obedience (enforcement), and IOED + dissonance (resistance-to-correction).**
- Status: **Mixed.** IOED: **documented, robust** (peer-reviewed). Conformity/obedience as *applied to hiring*: **argued, not documented** (the lab paradigms are canonical but unverified-in-corpus and untransferred to hiring rooms).
- IOED evidence: Rozenblit & Keil 2002; Alter et al. 2010; Fernbach et al. 2013 — **peer-reviewed**, verified-in-corpus. The decision-maker is *confidently wrong*, not *uncertainly ignorant*, about the role's mechanism.
- Conformity/obedience evidence: Asch, Milgram, Bond & Smith, Burger, Dolinski, Meeus & Raaijmakers — **textbook-canonical but unverified-in-corpus this pass**, and lab-to-hiring-room transfer untested (R1 Claim I, R2 #11). Carry as **plausible mechanism, not established for this domain.**

### What T2 still needs to be bulletproof
1. **The CFO/CMO verbatim comparison** — ✅ **DONE (2026-06-29).** CFO/CMO/COO verbatim JDs captured + coded (`2.1b_COMPARATOR_CODING_RESULTS.md`). Result: nothing about the JD-composite is CTO-specific — it is **C-suite-wide** (G-1 resolved). T2 is confirmed to be a claim about C-suite hiring artifacts generally. *(The narrower successor question — mode-separability — is now what "still needs" work; see G-1.)*
2. **A real JD sample with a denominator** — including mature-company JDs that *do* bound scope (R2 4D), to convert T2-2 from "absent in n≈2" to a rate.
3. **Peer-reviewed contemporary sourcing** — the entire T2 spine is content-site (R2 §2B). It carries descriptive weight only because the JD *is* the artifact under study; it cannot carry inferential weight about CTOs-in-general.

## PART 2 — T4: STRONGEST SURVIVING FORM

T4 asks why the culture grants "smart people can learn anything" to technical roles but withholds it from doctors/lawyers/accountants. The corpus's strongest, best-sourced material lives here (the 4.x historical tracks). But R2 4B showed the thought-experiment anchor is itself an availability artifact, and the master-variable answer is dead. Here is what survives.

### T4 surviving thesis

> The line the culture draws is **not capacity-drawn and not explained by any single master variable.** It tracks **whether a field installed legibility-and-accountability structure** — and that structure is installed by **multiple co-equal pathways** (named failure, complexity growth, market competition, jurisdictional competition, academic restructuring), **none necessary, none master.** Software sits where it does because **none** of these pathways has fired at the external-enforcement tier — not because one missing variable explains it.

### The load-bearing surviving claims, with honest confidence

**T4-1. Professional brakes install through *multiple* pathways; no single trigger is necessary.**
- Status: **Documented.** Confidence: **high** (this is the corpus's best-evidenced general claim).
- Evidence: Abbott (1988), *The System of Professions* — jurisdiction shifts via (1) failure/scandal, (2) complexity growth, (3) technology change, (4) jurisdictional competition. Corroborated by the corpus's own case spread: medicine (knowledge growth + military incentive, no named-failure trigger); actuarial/CAS 1914 (market — workers' comp); psychiatry (territorial); engineering (cumulative named failures — Tay Bridge, boilers, St. Francis Dam); accounting (named failure — Enron/WorldCom). (04 Finding 5; 04 Findings 1–4.)
- Source class: **peer-reviewed monograph** (Abbott) + **peer-reviewed** case sources (PubMed, Engineering Failure Analysis) + **named institution** (ABMS, FAA, SEC).
- This is the multi-pathway claim that *replaces* the dead master variable. It is more defensible than anything the corpus tried to coronate.

**T4-2. Liability is NOT the master enforcement variable — it is one of several co-equal, interdependent instruments.**
- Status: **Documented and genuinely falsified-then-corrected** (R2 #4 — the model exposure).
- Evidence: malpractice predated specialty boards 50–80 yrs without causing them; guild/college self-regulation predated tort standard-of-care ~250 yrs and tort *defers* to professional norms (Bolam 1957); aviation's ASRS runs on *immunity*; GARA *removed* a liability burden; EU CRA installs software brakes by *regulatory mandate*; Kolstad-Ulen-Johnson 1990 + Faure 2014 — liability is unreliable alone, a complement to regulation + insurance. (03 §3.9 Findings 1–8.)
- Source class: **peer-reviewed** (PMC, AER, J. Risk Research) + **primary regulation** (14 CFR 91.25; EU CRA).
- Confidence: **high.** This survives R1 and R2 intact and is one of the corpus's two model exposures.

**T4-3. The "smart generalist is fungible across roles" prior has a documented pre-software origin in administrative-management theory.**
- Status: **Documented at the origin node; the transmission to the modern CTO is a gap.**
- Evidence: Fayol (via Bedeian & Wren, peer-reviewed; Hatchuel & Segrestin 2019, peer-reviewed) — administration is general, teachable, and dominates technical specialty *at the top of the hierarchy*; "a good administrator but technically mediocre is more useful than a brilliant technician but mediocre administrator." Weber's office-not-person bureaucrat is an independent second channel. (04 §4.1b Findings 1–5.)
- Source class: **peer-reviewed** + **primary** (Fayol quotations).
- **Hard limits (R1 Claim F, R2 #7):**
  - The downstream transmission (Fayol → HBS/Chandler → modern CTO) is **unverified** (spend-limit killed the vote at 0-0). "Descends from Fayol" holds at origin only; the path to the CTO is **not a chain, it is a gap** — and may be Axis-2 convergent re-derivation, not lineage. The corpus's own broken-citation skepticism (applied to Axis 10) must be applied here symmetrically.
  - Fayol's administrator is **post-technical by design**; the CTO composite is **technical-and-everything-else**. Fayol does not deliver the *technical-retention* element that makes the CTO distinctive. So Fayol is at most the ancestor of the *generalist-executive* prior, **not** of the CTO-specific composite.
- Surviving form: "The generalist-executive prior is old and has a documented theoretical origin (Fayol/Weber); whether the *modern CTO* inherited it or convergently re-derived it is open."

**T4-4. The cross-generational software-failure pattern is amnesia (Axis 2), not akrasia (Axis 10) — at low confidence.**
- Status: **Argued** (from a weak instrument). Confidence: **low** (demoted from the Axis summary's confident prose, per R1 Claim G / R2 #8).
- Evidence: NATO 1968, Brooks 1975, CHAOS 1994 form no citation chain (CHAOS has no bibliography — the one firm node). (03 §3.10.)
- Source class: **secondary** (about whether primaries cite each other; primaries inaccessible) + one firm content/secondary node (CHAOS no-bibliography).
- **Limit:** citation is the wrong instrument for practitioner memory (transmitted via mentorship/training/ambient culture without citation). The reframe is plausible and honestly flagged, but it is **two inferential steps from the question.** Carry as low-confidence, not resolved.
- The *intra-generational* akrasia case is firmer: Brooks/CHAOS are cited *within* the current generation (in PMP/MBA curricula) and practice does not change. That narrower akrasia claim survives better than the cross-generational one.

**T4-5. The composite demand concentrates on high contributors — via citizenship pressure / CCB, driven by involuntariness not volume.**
- Status: **Documented as a general OB result; the link to the CTO specifically is broken** (R1 Claim J, R2 Part 3).
- Evidence: Bolino et al. 2010 (citizenship pressure, n=245); Vigoda-Gadot 2006 (CCB); Frontiers 2023 meta (CCB↔burnout ρ=0.71); Xu et al. 2021 (inverted-U: highest contributors deplete *least*); De Clercq et al. 2021 (involuntariness → depletion). (04 §4.8 Findings 4–5.)
- Source class: **peer-reviewed** throughout (the strongest-sourced loading claim).
- **The break to carry honestly:** the inverted-U severs "lands on the most capable and breaks them." The surviving mechanism — involuntariness — can land on anyone and is *least* plausible for a high-agency, high-status CTO. So this survives as a **general organizational-behavior result**, **not** as a mechanism that specifically explains CTO burnout. Do not re-attach it to capability. Use it for "discretionary contribution becomes felt obligation under authority pressure" — and stop there.

**T4-6. Programming was reclassified from specialized (often female) work to a generalist (masculine) template, installing a capability-signal credential.**
- Status: **Documented** (the historical reclassification), with the **dominance** claim correctly bounded by Misa.
- Evidence: Ensmenger 2010; Light 1999 (peer-reviewed); Abbate 2012; the Cannon-Perry circular profiling; PAT documented-invalid-yet-retained (an Axis-10-in-miniature instance). (03 §4.9.)
- Source class: **peer-reviewed** (Light) + **named academic press** (Ensmenger, Abbate, both MIT Press) + **secondary**.
- Surviving form: "Specialized work was performed, classified as clerical, and the reclassification machinery installed a *capability-signal* credential ('programmer'/'software engineer') that carries no scope boundary." This does **not** require women having been numerically dominant (Misa's challenge), and the corpus correctly concedes that.
- Connects to T4 cleanly: the *title-as-compression-artifact* mechanism ("software engineer" imported engineering's authority without its liability/credentialing/scope) is the same legibility failure as T2-2/T2-3, with a historical instance.

### What T4 still needs to be bulletproof
1. **The Fayol downstream transmission** (or its honest re-labeling as convergent re-derivation).
2. **A negative control** (R2 4C): a field with software's vulnerability profile that does *not* show the pathology. Without it, the profile is fitted to the outcome, not predictive. Science is treated as a confirming cousin, not a control.
3. **A non-extreme version of the doctor thought experiment** (R2 4B): the rural GP / physician-CEO / surgeon-entrepreneur shows the medical brake is *partial*, and the bright-line "instantly absurd" intuition is an availability artifact (the most extreme list was chosen). T4's anchor should be stated as "the brake in medicine is *stronger and more legible* than in software," not "the doctor composite is impossible."

## PART 4 — THE IRREDUCIBLE CORE

The minimal set of claims that, **if true, carry T2 and T4** — each with what it still needs to be bulletproof. This is what the synthesis should be built on, and nothing in the Graveyard may prop it up.

### Core claim 1 (carries T2): The legibility gap is real and located in the artifact, not the role.
> The CTO role is stage-contingent (T2-1); the hiring artifact applies the standard context-free and omits the trade-off (T2-2/T2-3); practitioner commentary names the trade-off the artifact omits (T2-3). The irrationality is the **context-free application of a context-dependent standard, with failure attributed to the individual** — a legibility/attribution failure, never a capacity claim.
- **Confidence: moderate**, on a **content-site evidence base** (named-practitioner at best).
- **Still needs:** (a) a real JD sample with a denominator including scope-bounded JDs; (b) at least one peer-reviewed contemporary source; (c) ~~to remain silent on CTO-specificity~~ → **RESOLVED 2026-06-29: Core 1 is confirmed to be about C-suite hiring artifacts generally — CTO-specificity is FALSE (G-1 closed), so Core 1 may now state the C-suite-wide scope affirmatively rather than hedging.**

### Core claim 2 (carries T4): Brakes install by multiple co-equal pathways; software has fired none at the external tier.
> No single master variable explains the line (G-2 dead). Abbott's multi-pathway model (T4-1) does: failure, complexity, market, jurisdiction, academic restructuring — none necessary. Liability specifically is one co-equal instrument, not master (T4-2). Software has reached internal self-governance (Tier 2) but no pathway has installed external enforcement (Tiers 3–4); the ACM/IEEE professionalization attempt failed; absorption via adjacent licensed professions (DO-178C, IEC 62443, ISO 26262) is the live non-failure pathway.
- **Confidence: high** for the multi-pathway model and the liability demotion (peer-reviewed); **moderate** for software's tier position (named institution + content site).
- **Still needs:** a negative control (R2 4C) to make the vulnerability profile predictive rather than fitted.

### Core claim 3 (carries T4, supports T2): The reflection gap is a robust DIAGNOSTIC of the applied-vs-endorsed gap — not a lever.
> Demanding a *mechanism* explanation ("by what process does one person hold all three modes seed→scale?") reliably drops felt understanding (IOED). This *exposes* that the judge cannot explain the standard they enforce — which is exactly the applied-vs-endorsed gap T2 locates. It does **not** reliably *moderate* the expectation (moderation failed three preregistered replications, is value-bounded, untested on role beliefs).
- **Confidence: high** that IOED is robust and mechanism-specific (peer-reviewed: Rozenblit & Keil; Alter; Fernbach; Crawford & Ruscio; Sloman & Vives; Zeveney & Marsh). **The diagnostic's transfer to identity-laden *role* beliefs is itself untested** (R1 Claim H) — so even the diagnostic carries a flag, not just the dead lever.
- **Still needs:** a direct test that the felt-understanding drop transfers to role/hiring beliefs (not just policy/voting), given the genius-composite may be identity-held (Axis 0/7/8) where explanation can bounce off.

### Core claim 4 (supports T4): The "smart generalist" prior is old, and the title is a compression artifact.
> The generalist-executive prior has a documented theoretical origin (Fayol/Weber — T4-3) and the historical reclassification of programming shows the title-as-compression-artifact mechanism (T4-6): a credential that signals general capability and carries no scope boundary. This is the same legibility failure as Core 1, with a pre-software instance.
- **Confidence: high** at the origin nodes (peer-reviewed); **the lineage to the modern CTO is a gap** (G-5), so this claim supports T4 as "the prior is old and the mechanism recurs," **not** as "the CTO inherited it from Fayol."
- **Still needs:** the downstream transmission verified, or the claim re-labeled as convergent re-derivation.

**How the core carries the theses without the graveyard:**
- T2 is carried by Core 1 + Core 3 (a documented legibility/attribution gap, plus a robust diagnostic that makes it visible) — **no** Builder/Multiplier/Strategist validation (G-3), **no** CTO-specificity (G-1), **no** un-co-holdability (G-6) required.
- T4 is carried by Core 2 + Core 4 (multi-pathway brake installation, software fired none; old prior + title-compression mechanism) — **no** master variable (G-2), **no** Fayol-to-CTO chain (G-5), **no** monotonic burnout (G-8) required.
