# Abstract — Cultural Expectations

*Folder-level abstract. Summarizes this area's result for a reader who wants the claim before deciding whether to read further. For the findings abstract this is based on, see [`2.1d_COMPARATOR_ABSTRACT.md`](2.1d_COMPARATOR_ABSTRACT.md) ("start here"); for the synthesis to build on, see [`DRAFT_SYNTHESIS_A.md`](DRAFT_SYNTHESIS_A.md); for the fence this area exists to guard, see [`DRAFT_SYNTHESIS_COMPARE.md`](DRAFT_SYNTHESIS_COMPARE.md).*

---

This area asks a different question than the other three: not "is the CTO composite demand real," but *where does the "unreasonableness" attributed to it actually live, and why does the culture grant "smart people can learn anything" to technical roles while withholding it from medicine, law, and aviation?* The doctor thought-experiment names the intuition precisely: everyone instantly recognizes that asking one doctor to perform surgery, run colonoscopies, file the practice's taxes, drive the ambulance, and design its next facility is absurd — not because any one task is too hard, but because the culture refuses to pile incompatible demands on a licensed role. It grants that same pile-up to software without a second thought. Eleven candidate structural axes (tangibility of complexity, institutional memory, liability, feedback-loop integrity, halo-bounded authority, and others) were developed to explain why the brake fires for doctors and fails for CTOs; they stack in causal order rather than operating independently, and the clearest single mechanism is that software's vocabulary is *wild-originated* — it carries a capability signal ("CTO" = technically excellent) but no scope boundary, unlike formally-originated professional language that carries scope and accountability together.

The area ran its own adversarial pass against itself, including a direct test of whether the CTO composite is actually CTO-specific. It is not: a matched-JD comparator program found every captured C-suite role (CFO, CMO, COO) demands an unbounded composite of its own three modes — the CFO's own JD names its version explicitly ("reconciling a borrowing-base certificate Wednesday, presenting to the board Thursday"). That result *disproves* the romantic, stronger-sounding claim ("the CTO is uniquely burdened") and *strengthens* the underlying findings by generalizing them: the composite-demand pattern (T2, the legibility/attribution gap in hiring artifacts) and the withheld-permission pattern (T4, where "smart → learn anything" is granted tracks whether a field ever installed external accountability structure) are general, industry-usable claims that don't need the CTO to be true. What survives as genuinely CTO-distinctive is narrower and better-evidenced than the original framing: the CTO is the only C-suite chief staffed *during* the individual-contributor phase (at founding, the product *is* the technology), and its craft churns and must be periodically re-earned rather than banked the way the CFO's accounting craft can be delegated permanently. The corrected framing: the CTO is **unique, not isolated** — the clearest use case of a pattern that runs the whole C-suite, not a one-off anomaly.

**The discipline this area holds to.** Every verdict lands on the *expectation* or the *process*, never on the *person, group, or belief* — the fence the area is built to guard, tested directly in the A/B synthesis comparison: the stronger-sounding "the composite is irrational — no one can hold three modes at once" is a capacity claim about people, unfalsifiable as stated, and one pronoun away from becoming a verdict on a specific failed CTO. The weaker-sounding but defensible form — "the standard is applied context-free to a role everyone's own commentary calls stage-dependent, and the resulting failure is misattributed to the individual" — is falsifiable, cannot be weaponized the same way, and is what the corpus builds on. Master-variable claims were tested and mostly retired rather than kept for rhetorical force (liability was disconfirmed as the master enforcement variable; Price's Law was refuted in its own home domain); evidence strength is tagged on every surviving claim rather than presented uniformly.
