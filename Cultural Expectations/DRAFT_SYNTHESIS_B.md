## §0 — The thesis in one paragraph (taxonomy-faithful form)

<!-- [⚠ WEAPONIZATION — §1.4 + §4.1] The thesis opens on the bare word "irrational" as the umbrella
     verdict. Taxonomy §1.4 forbids exactly this: the umbrella must be "not-reasonable," because
     "not-rational" names the whole set after one of its parts AND reads as a verdict on a *mind*.
     §4.1 confirms it mechanically: "irrational" is a migrating word — it completes "you are ___."
     THE SLIDE: a reader who accepts "the expectation is irrational" has already been handed the
     sentence "he was irrational to take the job." The umbrella word did the targeting before any
     argument was made. Draft A neutralizes this by using "not-reasonable" as the umbrella. -->
The expectation placed on the CTO is **irrational** in the taxonomy's precise sense (§1, §5): it
demands an output — simultaneous excellence as hands-on Builder, people-leading Multiplier, and
board-level Strategist — from **incompatible inputs**. Per the specimen matrix, this sits at
**Irrational, not Illogical**: occupying three mutually exclusive cognitive modes *at the same time*
<!-- [⚠ WEAPONIZATION — §4, the realm fence] "You cannot be in three states at once" is the load-
     bearing clause, and its grammatical subject is *you* — a person, not the expectation. The
     verdict is printed on the Expectation row but its truth-condition is a fact about human
     cognitive capacity. §4 calls this the diagonal: risk = severity-of-Word × commitment-of-Realm,
     and a capacity claim about a person is the bottom row wearing the top row's label. THE SLIDE:
     "the role demanded the impossible" → "he was foolish/arrogant to believe he could" — the
     attribution lands on the individual who failed, which is precisely the misattribution loop the
     research set out to expose, now reproduced by the research's own verdict. -->
is a physical/biological wall (you cannot be in three states at once), not a self-contradiction. The
demand is logically coherent but **magic-thinks** the cognitive substrate it runs on. The broader
cultural line — why software gets the "smart generalist is fungible" license while medicine, law, and
accounting do not — is that software never installed the scope-bounding **brakes** those professions
did, so the irrational composite is issued as an ordinary requirement and never corrected. The belief
"smart people can learn anything" is the upstream cultural fact that installs the composite
expectation; the framework **describes and traces** that belief but issues its verdict only on the
**downstream expectation**.

## §1 — Where the CTO composite lands on Axis B *(the verdict)*

**Claim:** The CTO composite expectation is **Irrational** — impossible *in practice* (the §1 wall
between "irrational = incompatible/absent inputs" and "unreasonable = real but context-infeasible").

<!-- [⚠ WEAPONIZATION — §1.3 + §5] This bullet works to push the case OFF the negotiable middle
     ("Unreasonable") and INTO the hard-wall cluster ("Irrational/Illogical"). Why that matters as a
     weapon: §1.3 says the negotiable middle "shades toward Reasonable" — an extra pair of hands
     rescues it. Forcing the case to the hard wall removes that exit, so the only remaining account
     of a failure is the *person* (more time/money "do nothing" → it must have been him). The
     classification choice itself decides whether failure is read as a fixable resourcing problem or
     as an indictment. Draft A keeps the case in the "Unreasonable" (capacity/context) zone, which
     preserves the rescue and keeps the verdict off the individual. -->
- **Why Irrational and not Unreasonable:** Unreasonable is the *negotiable middle* (§1.3) — a
  different deadline or an extra pair of hands moves it back across the line. The CTO composite is not
  rescued by negotiation: more time and more money do not let one person occupy the Builder, Multiplier,
  and Strategist cognitive modes *simultaneously*. That non-negotiability is the signature of the hard
  wall, which places it with Irrational/Illogical, not with Unreasonable.
- **Why Irrational and not Illogical:** there is no *logical* contradiction in the job description —
  the floor (Illogical) is reserved for demands with literally no satisfying assignment in any world.
  Three cognitive modes are individually satisfiable; the wall is the *simultaneity*, a
  physical/biological limit. (Taxonomy §5 note, verbatim.)
- **Source for the verdict:** the taxonomy's own specimen matrix, row "CTO as IC + Manager +
  Strategist, simultaneously → **Irrational** → requires mutually exclusive cognitive modes at the same
  time → context-switching thrashing."

> **This is the load-bearing move of Draft B — and the one the adversarial pass attacks.** The verdict
> "Irrational" rests on a claim about *what one person's cognition can hold* (the role/capacity). The
> COMPARE file shows this is exactly the subject-swap R1 flagged: a verdict that reads as on-the-
> expectation but reduces to on-the-role-design.

## §2 — The composite is the default framing of the hiring artifact *(T2 spine)*

**Claim:** The irrational composite is the **default** framing of the CTO JD — three modes presented
as one unified responsibility set, stage-modulated, with the trade-off named only in *commentary*,
never in the artifact that hires.

- **Evidence:** the 2.1 JD pass (Wellfound; SPMB Architect→Scaler→Strategic-Operator archetypes;
  startups.com engineer-count bands; Camille Fournier).
- **Source class:** content site / recruiter template / named exec-search / named practitioner. **No
  peer-reviewed source** — these are the behavioral artifacts under study.
- **The mechanism:** the JD compresses the trade-off *out* — the lossy compression the framework
  predicts. The knowledge that the composite is irrational exists in practitioner commentary and does
  not travel to the decision artifact.

> Draft B states the composite as CTO-typical without hedging the specificity. (Draft A demotes this to
> "C-suite hiring artifacts generally" pending CFO/CMO comparison — see COMPARE.)

## §3 — The composite is socially produced and enforced *(T2 mechanism)*

**Claim:** Three mechanisms generate, enforce, and protect the irrational composite.

- **Generation — conformity (Asch):** the composite is inherited convention, not individually
  reasoned; the JD is the Asch majority.
- **Enforcement — obedience (Milgram; Meeus & Raaijmakers):** evaluators enforce the composite under
  real authority despite private doubt.
<!-- [⚠ WEAPONIZATION — §4 + §4.1] "The decision-maker is confidently wrong" moves the verdict from
     the expectation onto a *person in the room* (the evaluator), and "confidently wrong" is a
     characterization phrase that completes "you are ___." This is the second-pillar slide the
     language layer guards (common_vs_normal / failure_language_framework): a finding about a
     cognitive illusion (IOED is a property of how explanation works, true of everyone) gets
     rephrased as a property of *this evaluator's* judgment. THE SLIDE: "IOED affects all of us" →
     "the hiring manager was confidently wrong" → "the hiring manager is incompetent." Keep IOED on
     the mechanism ("the standard is enforced under an illusion of understanding"), never on the
     enforcer as a person. -->
- **Resistance-to-correction — IOED + dissonance:** the decision-maker is *confidently wrong* about how
  the composite is held (Rozenblit & Keil; Alter; Fernbach), and dissonance strengthens the standard
  under failure.
- **The lever:** Asch + Milgram converge — one credible dissenter collapses compliance. Draft B
  presents the dissenter as the **robust intervention**.

> Draft B leads with the conformity/obedience material and calls the dissenter "robust." (Draft A
> demotes the dissenter to an unverified hypothesis and notes the framework has **no** verified lever —
> see COMPARE; this is the second place Draft B's confidence outruns its evidence.)

## §4 — The reflection gap exposes the irrationality *(T2 locator)*

**Claim:** Asking the decision-maker to explain the *mechanism* — "by what process does one person hold
all three modes seed→scale?" — reveals they cannot, exposing the composite as irrational and
unexamined.

- **Evidence:** 5.6 / 2.4 (Rozenblit & Keil; Alter; Zeveney & Marsh). The felt-understanding drop is
  robust; Draft B reads it as evidence the composite *is* irrational.
- Draft B treats the reflection gap as both diagnostic and (via the dissenter) a route to correction.

> Draft A splits these hard: the reflection gap is a **diagnostic only** (moderation failed three
> preregistered replications), and it is the *dissenter*, not the reflection gap, that was proposed as
> the lever — then demoted. Draft B blurs the diagnostic/lever line. (COMPARE.)

## §5 — Why the irrational composite is never corrected *(T4)*

general, teachable, dominates technical specialty at the top); Weber's office-not-person bureaucrat is

- **Evidence:** 4.1b (Fayol — Bedeian & Wren; Hatchuel & Segrestin); 3.10 (broken NATO/Brooks/CHAOS
