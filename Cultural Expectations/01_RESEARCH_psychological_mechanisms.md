## The three mechanisms, mapped to T2

### 1. How the expectation is GENERATED — and why no one need have reasoned it out

**Asch / conformity (5.2).** The composite expectation does not require any individual to have derived it from first principles. It is **socially reproduced**: each hiring room inherits the composite framing from the last, and the unanimity of "this is what a CTO is" overrides any individual's private doubt. 2.1 supplies the artifact that carries the convention — the JD that frames all three modes as one unified set and never names the tension. The JD *is* the Asch majority: it presents the composite as the consensus answer, and the individual evaluator conforms to it on a cross-domain question ("what should a CTO be able to do?") where their own expertise doesn't directly apply.

**Availability / the genius archetype (5.4, deferred but noted).** The composite is also the *available* prototype (Axis 0). The evaluator reaches for the vivid, retrievable image (the genius founder-CTO who does everything) rather than the abstract base rate. Track 5 deferred 5.4 as enrichment, but the availability mechanism is why the *content* of the conformity convention is the composite specifically.

### 2. How the expectation is ENFORCED — and why private doubt doesn't stop it

**Milgram / obedience (5.1).** ⚑ *(Unverified-in-corpus — canonical figures.)* The evaluator who privately suspects the composite is unreasonable still enforces it because the **authority applying it is real** — the board, the CEO, the market consensus. Meeus & Raaijmakers (1986) situate this in an evaluation/hiring-adjacent context: people impose harm in administrative-evaluation settings when an authority directs it. The composite is enforced not because evaluators believe it but because the structure asks them to and refusal feels unavailable.

**The convergence with Asch — the framework's lever.** Both paradigms point to the *same* intervention: **one credible dissenter breaking the perceived unanimity collapses compliance/conformity.** This is the framework's most actionable prediction — one credible person in the hiring room naming the composite as unreasonable. Track 5's strategic finding: **this is the ROBUST leg of the intervention** (pending number-verification), and it should lead the framework's "what would change the answer" account — *not* the reflection gap.

### 3. Why the expectation RESISTS correction — and what the reflection gap can and can't do

**IOED / Fernbach (5.6).** The decision-maker operates under an **illusion of explanatory depth** about the role: they have observed the outputs (product ships, team grows) without understanding the mechanism, and they are confidently wrong rather than uncertainly ignorant. Asking them to explain the *mechanism* ("by what process does one person hold all three modes seed→scale?") reliably **reveals the gap** — this is the reflection-gap diagnostic, and it is empirically robust.

⚑ **But — the load-bearing limit from 5.6:** revealing the gap does **not** reliably **moderate** the expectation. The moderation effect failed three preregistered replications (Crawford & Ruscio 2021), works only for consequentialist (not identity/value) beliefs (Sloman & Vives 2022), and produces *diffuse* humility rather than targeted correction (Zeveney & Marsh 2023). Since the genius-composite is plausibly an identity/value belief (Axis 0/7/8), explanation may bounce off entirely.

**Cognitive dissonance / Festinger (5.5, deferred but noted).** This is *why* the expectation strengthens under disconfirmation: a CEO who has applied the composite standard and watched CTOs fail resolves the dissonance by strengthening the standard ("we need a *better* CTO") rather than questioning it. Repetition reinforces the standard rather than eroding it — which is why the reflection gap, even when it fires, faces an active counter-pressure.

## The integrated T2 picture

| Stage | Mechanism | T2 manifestation | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Generation** | Asch conformity + availability | The composite is the inherited, vivid default; no one reasons it out (the JD carries it — 2.1) | 5.2 unverified-in-corpus; pattern strong |

**The T2 thesis, mechanism-grounded:** the irrationality lives in the *expectation/attribution* locus, and three documented mechanisms keep it there — conformity generates it, obedience enforces it, IOED+dissonance protect it from correction. The framework's exit is the **dissenter** (robust), with the **reflection gap as the diagnostic that makes the irrationality visible** (robust as a diagnostic, unproven as a lever).
