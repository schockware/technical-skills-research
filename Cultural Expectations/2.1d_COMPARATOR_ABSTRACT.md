# Abstract — The C-Suite Comparator Program: Is the Composite CTO-Specific?

*Findings abstract of the 2026-06-28/29 comparator arc. Reports what the research established and how
well-supported each piece is. No opinion; evidence strength carried on every claim. Register (private
anchor vs. shareable summary) to be set after review.*

---

**Question.** The corpus held that the CTO role demands an unbounded *composite* — deep technical craft
+ people leadership + board/strategy, simultaneously — and treated this as distinctive of the CTO. That
specificity was untested (gap G-1): the live alternative was that *all* C-suite roles are framed as
composites. This program captured matched-stage non-CTO C-suite job descriptions and coded them
frame-neutrally to decide it, then pursued what — if anything — remains distinctive about the CTO.

**Method.** Nine comparator JDs (CFO, CMO, COO × three growth stages) were captured verbatim from
Wellfound, source-matched to the existing seven-JD CTO sample, and coded against each role's *own* three
modes (craft / people / strategy) — never the CTO's bins, to avoid a rigged comparison. A pre-registered
decision rule fixed the verdict map before coding. The COO served as a pre-registered control (a known
catch-all that *must* score composite, or the method is declared broken). Findings were cross-checked
against four verified deep-research passes (adversarial claim-verification; sources rated).

**Primary finding — G-1 resolved: CTO-specificity is FALSE; the composite is C-suite-wide.** [strong]
Every captured C-suite JD demanded ≥2 (typically 3) of its own modes, unbounded. CFO scored composite
3/3 — including the supposedly most-bounded role, which named its own composite explicitly
("player-coach… reconciling a borrowing-base certificate Wednesday, presenting to the board Thursday").
The COO control fired 4/4 across four role-flavors and all stages, including a bounded government grade —
validating the method. No comparator at any stage scored bounded. *(Limit: Wellfound-only content-site
source class; small n per cell; CMO early/growth cells documented-structural, not captured.)*

**Secondary finding — the distinctiveness question is three questions, and the CTO is distinctive on only
one axis.** [mixed strength, per axis]
- *Axis 1 — composite present:* C-suite-wide; NOT distinctive. [strong — the G-1 coding]
- *Axis 2 — the IC→manager→executive career rupture:* a UNIVERSAL "master-craftsman-becomes-executive"
  pattern (documented in sales [Benson/Li/Shue, QJE 2019], surgery, academia, general management); NOT
  distinctive to the CTO. The "identity-rupture makes the CTO special" rationale is **refuted.** [strong —
  verified, multi-domain]
- *Axis 3 — role-scaling / stage:* CTO-DISTINCTIVE. [structural argument; supporting data soft] A chief
  title exists to *lead a function*; a function must outgrow individual-contributor work before it can be
  led; therefore every chief is staffed *post-IC* — except the CTO, staffed *during* the IC phase because
  at founding the product *is* the technology. The CTO is the only chief assigned to an IC-phase role,
  hence the only one that must traverse the role's stage-migration *in situ* rather than being hired in
  fully formed. Supporting (non-statistical): CTO JD mode-balance migrates across stages (craft-mode
  dominant at seed → near-zero at scale) while the CFO's is stable.

**Mechanism — why the CTO craft can't be shed.** [partial: strong on CTO/CFO, inferred beyond] The CTO
*re-fuses* its craft rather than banking it: engineering skill decays (~2–3 yr onset, ~5 yr credibility
ceiling — Majors, named author), so the leader must periodically return to hands-on work; a CTO who
delegates everything becomes a figurehead. The CFO, by contrast, *banks* its craft — accounting divides
into durable Controller/FP&A modes the CFO permanently delegates and is even incentivized to climb past.
Career-entry confirms the asymmetry from the other end: the CFO frequently enters laterally (≤31%
accounting-craft route-up; MBA 47–51% > CPA 35–38%, Spencer Stuart census), while the CTO is expected to
have come up through the craft ("one step removed from writing code"). *(Asymmetry: CFO well-quantified;
CTO qualitative; COO essentially unstudied.)*

**Commensurability result — the distinctiveness and the comparison difficulty are the same phenomenon.**
[structural reading] Across twelve dimensions, the CTO is distinctive exactly where the four roles are
*not* commensurable (craft decays-vs-banks; present-from-founding; craft-anchored identity) and ordinary
on the one dimension where they *are* commensurable (the universal identity rupture). "Is the CTO
special?" presumed a shared ruler; the supported answer is: *special in the ways the roles cannot be put
on one ruler, ordinary in the one way they can.* A consequence is a research-foundation **disqualifier**:
cross-role claims using a shared word for non-shared substances are unsupported — e.g., the ~50% IC→EM
performance-regression figure is tech-only and has no non-CTO equivalent, so it cannot be used
comparatively.

**On the second thesis (T4 — "smart people can learn anything," granted to software, withheld from
licensed professions).** [status, not new finding] T4 is intact and is the better-evidenced thesis, but
it currently explains the *institutional consequence* (which fields installed external accountability
brakes — multi-pathway, no master variable) rather than the *belief itself*. A candidate
belief-plausibility mechanism surfaced but is not yet adopted: software's churning, non-bankable craft
makes "just learn it" *look* true (everyone is perpetually re-learning; seniority is no moat), whereas
compounding, bankable professional craft makes it *look* false. This stays a *description* of why the
belief is held, never a verdict on the belief (realm-fence discipline).

**Open gaps (ranked).** [explicit] (1) COO is under-evidenced on every dimension but the composite. (2)
No IC→EM regression rate exists for any non-CTO role — the corpus's hardest number is unpaired. (3)
CMO/COO craft-decay unmeasured (the decay contrast is 2-of-4). (4) CTO feeder-path census missing (CFO
has one — asymmetric proof). (5) T4's belief-plausibility leg is a candidate, scoped to plausibility not
truth, evidence 2-of-N.

**Bottom line — what was actually proved.** The findings are **T2 and T4**, and they stand on their own:
T2 (the legibility/attribution gap in hiring artifacts — a context-free standard applied to a
stage-contingent role, failure mis-attributed to the individual) and T4 (where the "smart → learn
anything" belief is granted tracks whether a field installed external accountability structure). Both
are general claims about how organizations compress roles and grant competence beliefs; neither needs
the CTO to be true.

**The CTO is the *use case*, not the subject.** It is the worked example that makes T2 and T4 legible —
the role where the pattern shows up most vividly (composite from founding, churning un-bankable craft,
the only chief staffed during the IC phase). The corpus was originally organized with the CTO *as* the
subject; this program inverts that — T2/T4 are the subject, the CTO is the cleanest demonstration
specimen.

**The precise correction: the CTO is UNIQUE, not ISOLATED.** *Isolated* (the romantic form — "the CTO
is the only role this happens to") is **disproved**: the composite is C-suite-wide and the
career-rupture is universal. *Unique* (a genuinely distinctive instance of a general pattern) is
**proved**: axis-3 — the only chief staffed during the IC phase, the only craft that churns faster than
it can be banked. The romanticization's error was reading uniqueness *as* isolation; correcting it makes
T2/T4 **stronger** (general claims with a vivid exemplar), not weaker (claims about one peculiar role).

The contribution, then, is to make a difference that is *practically obvious* (the day-to-day work of a
CTO, CFO, CMO, COO is plainly different — shepherd vs. shearer vs. weaver) *legible* — to name the
characteristics the eye already sees but the artifacts (the "C-suite" category, the composite JD)
compress out. That legibility move is itself T2, turned on the comparison.
