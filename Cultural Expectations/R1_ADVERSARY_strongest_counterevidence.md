## THE THREE MOST DAMAGING ATTACKS (lead)

### LEAD ATTACK 1 — The expectation/role-design separation is not maintained; it is a relabeling. (Targets T2 core.) Severity: WOUNDING, trending FATAL for the *clean-separation* discipline rule.

T2's entire license is that a sibling pass "retired the role itself is irrational," so the irrationality must now live in the **expectation/attribution** locus, never in role design. The corpus repeatedly asserts it is honoring this. It is not. The load-bearing empirical spine of T2 (2.1) is that the JD "demands all three modes simultaneously," "never names the tension," and "compresses out" the conflict between Builder/Multiplier/Strategist. But "three modes that conflict and cannot be co-held by one person" **is a claim about the role's structure**, not about the standard a judge applies. The corpus smuggles role-design back in under an expectation label by the following move: it relocates the *same* impossibility claim from "the role is too big" to "the expectation that the role can be held is too big." Those are not different findings. They are the same finding with the subject of the sentence swapped.

The tell is everywhere in the axes. Axis 5/9's "four-tier brake," Axis 4's "no institutional body says the demand exceeds one person's scope," and the Compound section's "the unreasonable demand" all describe a demand whose unreasonableness is defined by *what the work actually requires* — i.e., by role design. Axis 1 (tangibility) is explicitly about "the cognitive architecture required for each mode is radically different" — a statement about the work, not about the judge. The corpus cannot have it both ways: if the three modes genuinely require incompatible cognitive states (role design), then the irrationality is at least partly in the role, and the sibling-track retirement is being quietly overridden. If the three modes do *not* require incompatible states, then 2.1's "composite demand" finding loses its force.

**What a skeptic says:** "You retired role-design irrationality by fiat in another track, then re-imported it here wearing an 'expectation' costume. The separation is rhetorical. Every place you say 'the expectation is unreasonable' you are relying on a premise about what the role actually demands."

**Counter-evidence / alternative framing:** The cleaner position — and one the corpus never adopts — is the **contingency-theory** reading (reasoning, not a single cite: this is the Lawrence & Lorsch / Mintzberg tradition). On that reading the CTO role is *not* a fixed composite at all; it is a role whose required mode-mix is set by stage and context (which 2.1 Finding 2 actually shows). If the role is stage-contingent, then "expecting all three at once" is not an irrational *standard* — it is a category error by *specific* evaluators who failed to localize the role to its stage. That makes the irrationality an *information/legibility* problem (Axis 6), not an *expectation* problem, and dissolves T2's framing entirely. The corpus's own Finding 2 (stage-modulation confirmed 3-0) is the strongest evidence *against* T2's "composite is the irrational default" framing, because it shows the field already knows the role is stage-specific.

**What the corpus must do to answer:** State explicitly and operationally what distinguishes "the role demands incompatible modes" (forbidden) from "the expectation demands incompatible modes" (permitted), at the level of a single testable sentence. If it cannot draw that line without reference to what the work requires, the separation fails and T2 must be re-scoped to a pure legibility/attribution claim — losing most of the axis stack, which is about the demand itself.

### LEAD ATTACK 2 — "Visible attributable harm" is the exact same single-master-variable over-reach the corpus claims to have learned from — and it is *less* tested than the variables it replaced. (Targets 3.9/4.7 candidate master variable.) Severity: WOUNDING.

The corpus is proud of having demoted three master-variable claims: Price's Law (4.8), liability-as-master (3.9), and "Taylor amplified the composite" (4.2). Having killed liability as the master variable, it immediately **coronates a replacement** — "visible attributable harm" — as "the candidate master variable behind all three brake types" (3.9 Finding revision; Axis 5/9 banners; 4.7). This is the precise error it just diagnosed, committed again in the same document.

Three problems, each independently serious:

1. **It is admitted to be untested.** 3.9 says verbatim: "No claim *directly* tested harm-visibility as the master variable." So the corpus retired a variable that had ~23 verified claims behind it (liability) and enthroned one with **zero** direct tests. By its own source-discipline that is a downgrade in evidentiary status dressed as an upgrade in explanatory power.

2. **It is unfalsifiable as stated.** "Visible attributable harm" is elastic enough to post-hoc fit every case: orthopedic deformities (medicine), production collapse (GA), named crashes (aviation), Enron (accounting). When a construct fits visible-deformity *and* a corporate bankruptcy *and* an industry's unit-volume decline, it is not a variable — it is a synonym for "a salient bad thing happened that people noticed." The corpus's own counter-cases bite here: medicine's specialty boards were driven by **knowledge growth and market competition** (block optometry), with *no* visible harm event in the 1916–17 founding (3.9 Finding 1; 4.7 Finding 4). Actuarial science (CAS 1914) was **market-driven** by workers'-comp, no harm event. Psychiatry was **territorial**. If three of the corpus's own brake-installation cases fired *without* visible attributable harm, then harm-visibility is not the master variable either — it is, at best, one more co-equal instrument, which is exactly the lesson 3.9 said it had learned about liability.

3. **The "absorption path" undercuts it.** 4.7/Axis 5 argue software is acquiring brakes via adjacent licensed professions (DO-178C, IEC 62443, ISO 26262) — i.e., **jurisdictional competition (Abbott)**, with no software-specific harm event. That is a fourth pathway with no harm trigger, conceded in the corpus, which directly contradicts harm-visibility-as-master.

**What a skeptic says:** "You learned nothing from killing the liability master variable. You just needed a master variable, so you minted one out of the residue and never tested it. Abbott (1988) — *your own load-bearing source* — explicitly says jurisdiction shifts through **four** mechanisms, of which failure/scandal is only one. Your refined claim contradicts your foundational citation."

**What the corpus must do to answer:** Either (a) demote "visible attributable harm" to one-of-several, symmetric with the liability demotion, and stop calling it the master variable; or (b) run the dedicated harm-visibility test it admits it never ran, *including* the disconfirming medicine/actuarial/psychiatry/absorption cases that already sit in its own files as counter-evidence.

### LEAD ATTACK 3 — The Builder/Multiplier/Strategist taxonomy is an imposed construct, found everywhere because it was coded into the search. (Targets the FRAME.) Severity: WOUNDING.

The three-mode taxonomy is the lens through which every JD in 2.1 is read — responsibilities are *assigned* to modes by the coder, mode distributions are tabulated, "composite-demand sentences" are selected. But the taxonomy has **no independent validation in the corpus**. It is never sourced to an organizational-psychology construct, a factor analysis, a competency model, or any external typology. It is asserted, then used as the coding frame, then "confirmed" because the coder can sort sentences into the three bins the coder defined. This is the textbook structure of a construct that is *imposed and then found everywhere* — the very risk the review prompt names.

The circularity is concrete: 2.1 Finding 1 ("composite is the default, presents modes as complementary") is **guaranteed** by the method. Any executive JD lists technical, people, and business responsibilities; binning them into three pre-chosen modes and observing that all three bins are non-empty is not a finding about CTOs — it is a property of the bins. The "no tension named" observation is similarly an artifact: JDs are aspirational marketing documents that *never* name tension for *any* role (a CFO JD does not say "and these duties conflict"). The corpus has no negative control proving the absence-of-tension is CTO-specific rather than generic-to-JDs.

Worse, the corpus's own best comparator **breaks the frame**: the CRO (2.1 Finding 6) is "cleanly scoped to leadership/strategy" with "zero IC requirement." If the three-mode composite were a real, discovered structure, it would either appear in the CRO or its absence would be explained by the taxonomy. Instead the taxonomy simply doesn't apply to the CRO — which suggests the modes are not a general competency structure but an ad-hoc description of one role, reverse-engineered to make that role look uniquely overloaded.

**What a skeptic says:** "You invented three boxes, sorted job duties into them, and announced that jobs have duties in all three boxes. That's not research; that's the coding scheme talking. Show me the Builder/Multiplier/Strategist construct validated *outside* this corpus, and show me a role that the frame predicts should be a non-composite — then test it blind."

**What the corpus must do to answer:** Ground the taxonomy in an external, validated competency framework (or admit it is a heuristic, not a discovered structure). Run blind double-coding with inter-rater reliability. Add a negative-control role the frame predicts is *non*-composite. Until then, every "composite confirmed" finding in 2.1 is method-driven and cannot bear weight.

## FULL CLAIM-BY-CLAIM PROSECUTION

### Claim A — T2 core: the irrationality lives in expectation/attribution, not role design.
See Lead Attack 1. **Severity: WOUNDING (FATAL for the clean-separation rule unless answered).** The separation is not operationalized; role-design impossibility is re-imported under an expectation label; the corpus's own stage-modulation finding points to a legibility explanation that dissolves the framing.

### Claim B — 2.1: the composite is the DEFAULT JD framing, stage-modulated, tension named only in commentary.
**Counter:** Two distinct sub-claims, distinct exposure.
- *Default framing / no tension in JD:* SURVIVABLE but soft. The mechanism is real-ish but the finding is partly a method artifact (Lead Attack 3) and rests entirely on **content-site / recruiter-template / jobs-platform** sources — the corpus admits "No peer-reviewed source appears in this corpus" for 2.1 and that confirmation was as thin as "two WebFetch passes" on a Wellfound page. A negative finding ("no such sentence exists") drawn by inspection of ~2 JDs is weak; the corpus rates it "medium" but even medium is generous for an n that small. **The "3-0" notation is misleading** — it reflects an internal verifier vote, not three independent high-quality sources; the underlying material is one or two content-site pages.
- *Stage-modulation:* SURVIVABLE. Best-supported leg (SPMB, startups.com), but engineer-count bands are "illustrative, not empirically derived" (admitted). Glancing weakness only.
- **The deeper problem:** stage-modulation **undercuts** the default-composite claim (Lead Attack 1). If the field publishes Architect→Scaler→Strategic-Operator archetypes, the field *already names the role as stage-specific* — so the "composite default that hides the tension" is contradicted by the corpus's own Finding 2.

### Claim C — 2.1: the composite is CTO-SPECIFIC.
**Counter: WOUNDING — and "under-powered" is NOT an honest enough label; the claim is effectively unsupported.** The corpus rests CTO-specificity on **one** verified comparator (CRO), with CMO refuted and no CFO surviving. One data point is not "under-powered"; it is "untested." A single comparator cannot distinguish "CTOs are special" from "the CRO happens to be cleanly scoped." The live alternative the corpus cannot exclude — that **all C-suite roles are composite** and the corpus simply failed to verify CFO/CMO composite evidence — is at least as consistent with the data. The honest status is: **CTO-specificity is unsupported, not merely under-powered.** The corpus half-admits this ("possible that all C-suite JDs are composite") but still lets the framing lean on CTO-uniqueness throughout the axes (Axis 7/8 "the CTO halos," the whole T2 framing). **The label launders an absence of evidence into a presence of weak evidence.**

### Claim D — 4.7: visible attributed failure triggers professional specialization (refined to "sufficient not necessary").
**Counter: SURVIVABLE in the refined form, but the refinement is so heavily qualified it barely constrains anything.** The corpus did the honest work here: it found medicine, actuarial, psychiatry, and the aviation founding Act all specialized *without* a named-failure trigger, and refined the claim to "sufficient not necessary; fastest/hardest brakes need named failure." That refined claim is defensible. **But:** "sufficient but not necessary, and also produces the hardest version" is close to unfalsifiable — almost any trigger theory survives "X is sufficient but not necessary." And the residual hard claim ("liability/external oversight specifically requires a named failure") is contradicted in the corpus's own files by **EU CRA installing software brakes via regulatory mandate with no named software catastrophe** (3.9 Finding 7). So even the residual is wounded. Net: the refined 4.7 claim holds only as a weak tendency, not a law.

### Claim E — 3.9/4.7: "visible attributable harm" is the candidate master variable replacing liability.
See Lead Attack 2. **Severity: WOUNDING.** Same over-reach the corpus claims to have outgrown; admittedly untested; contradicted by ≥3 of its own brake cases and by Abbott's four-mechanism model.

### Claim F — 4.1b: the composite generalist manager descends from Fayol, not Taylor.
**Counter: SURVIVABLE, with one real exposure.** This is among the corpus's better-evidenced claims (peer-reviewed Bedeian & Wren; Hatchuel & Segrestin), and the Fayol primary quotations genuinely support "administration is general, teachable, dominates technical at the top." Attacks:
1. **Downstream lineage is unverified** (admitted — spend-limit killed the HBS/Chandler/"invented tradition" vote at 0-0). So "descends from Fayol" is established at the *origin* node but **the transmission path to the modern CTO is a gap**, not a chain. The corpus proved Fayol *said* it; it did not prove the modern expectation *inherited* it from him rather than convergently re-deriving it (which would make this an Axis 2 amnesia case, not a lineage). This is the *same broken-citation-chain problem* that demoted Axis 10 in 3.10 — and the corpus does not apply that skepticism symmetrically to Fayol.
2. **Over-attribution risk:** "the CTO's composite ancestor is Fayol" is a strong directional claim built from a thinker who explicitly bounded his own claim to the *upper* hierarchy (the 0-3 refuted sub-claim). The corpus says this "sharpens" the lineage, but it equally means Fayol is talking about CEOs/general managers, **not** a *technical* officer expected to retain Builder craft — the very thing that makes the CTO composite distinctive. Fayol's administrator is *post-technical by design*; the CTO composite is *technical-and-everything-else*. That is a different beast, and the lineage may not actually deliver the CTO-specific composite at all.
**Verdict:** Fayol-as-origin: SURVIVABLE. Fayol-as-ancestor-of-the-CTO-composite-specifically: WOUNDING (the technical-retention element of the CTO composite is absent from Fayol).

### Claim G — 3.10: the cross-generational software pattern is Axis 2 (amnesia), not Axis 10 (akrasia), because the citation trail is broken.
**Counter: SURVIVABLE — and this is the corpus's most intellectually honest move — but the evidence base is thin and the conclusion may be an artifact of source access.** The finding rests on "no secondary source confirms Brooks cited NATO" and "CHAOS has no bibliography." **Absence of a citation in a trade report is weak evidence of absence of knowledge transmission.** Practitioners absorb prior findings through training, mentorship, and ambient professional culture without formal citation; the academic citation chain is the *wrong instrument* for measuring practitioner-community memory. The corpus itself flags "medium confidence on the NATO and Brooks findings pending primary source access" and that primary PDFs were inaccessible. So the headline reframe ("civilizational amnesia, not akrasia") is built on **secondary sources about whether primary sources cite each other** — two inferential steps from the actual question. A skeptic notes: Brooks is *universally* cited *within* the modern generation (admitted), which is the corpus's own definition of Axis 10; the "broken chain" is only at the cross-generational nodes, measured by citation presence, which is the weakest possible proxy. **Verdict:** the Axis 2/10 reclassification is plausible and honestly flagged, but it is a low-confidence finding masquerading (in the Axis summary's confident prose) as resolved.

### Claim H — 5.6/2.4: the reflection-gap test is a valid diagnostic of the applied-vs-endorsed gap.
**Counter: SURVIVABLE as a diagnostic — this is the best-disciplined section in the corpus — with two residual attacks.**
1. **The diagnostic's transfer to role/hiring beliefs is itself untested.** 5.6 establishes IOED transfers to *policy/voting* and is *theoretically* domain-general (Alter et al. say "might," social-domain transfer is "theoretical extrapolation"). The corpus correctly kills the *moderation* leg but lets the *diagnostic* leg ride on the same untested-on-role-beliefs extrapolation it flagged as fatal for moderation. If "explanation bounces off identity/value beliefs" (Sloman & Vives) and the genius-composite is plausibly an identity belief (the corpus's own Axis 0/7/8 claim), then the *felt-understanding drop itself* may also be blunted for this belief class — not just the moderation. The corpus assumes the diagnostic survives where the intervention dies, but provides no evidence the *diagnostic* transfers to identity-laden role beliefs specifically.
2. **2.4 is "constructed, not hunted"** (admitted: written as analysis on 5.6+4.7+2.1, "no separate research run"). So 2.4 is a *prediction*, not a *finding*. The corpus is clear about this, which is why it's only SURVIVABLE rather than wounded — but a reader skimming the Axis summary would not know 2.4 contains zero direct reflection-gap evidence from the CTO domain.

### Claim I — Track 1: the dissenter (Asch/Milgram convergence) is the robust intervention lever.
**Counter: WOUNDING.** The "robust" leg is built on figures the corpus itself marks **UNVERIFIED-IN-CORPUS** — Milgram, Asch, Burger, Dolinski, Bond & Smith were *not* run through the adversarial verifier this pass. The corpus calls this the "robust leg," but its robustness is asserted from textbook canon, not from the corpus's own verification discipline, while the *weaker* (reflection-gap) leg got the full verification. So the corpus's confidence ordering is **inverse to its evidence ordering**: it leads with the leg it did NOT check and hedges the leg it DID. Two further attacks:
- **External validity of the dissenter effect to a hiring room is untested.** Asch (line judgments) and Milgram (shock obedience) are laboratory paradigms; "one credible dissenter in a CTO hiring panel changes the composite expectation" is a large extrapolation across context, stakes, and group type. Meeus & Raaijmakers (administrative obedience) is the closest analog and is one study, unverified here.
- **The dissenter mechanism assumes the composite is held via conformity/obedience.** If the composite is instead held as a genuine identity belief (Axis 0/7/8) — which the corpus argues elsewhere to explain why the reflection gap bounces off — then a single dissenter should *also* bounce off, for the same reason. The corpus invokes identity-belief stickiness to protect one finding (moderation fails) and ignores it for another (dissenter works). **It cannot have identity-belief-resistance both ways.**

### Claim J — 4.8: composite-demand concentration via citizenship pressure / CCB (the re-founded claim).
**Counter: SURVIVABLE, with a clean internal contradiction the corpus surfaced but did not fully metabolize.** The re-founding on CCB (Bolino, Vigoda-Gadot) is a genuine upgrade and well-cited. But the corpus's own Finding 5 (inverted-U; highest contributors show *less* fatigue, Xu 2021) **breaks the link to the CTO case**: if the most capable contributors deplete *least*, then the "composite lands on the most capable and breaks them" story — which the whole CTO-burnout framing needs — is exactly the monotonic story the evidence refutes. The corpus rescues this by pivoting to "involuntariness, not volume," but that pivot **severs the connection to capability**: involuntariness can land on anyone, high or low contributor. So the re-founded claim survives as a general OB result but **no longer specifically explains why the *CTO* (a high-agency, high-status role) is the victim** — a CTO is the *least* plausibly "no choice" actor in the org. This is a quiet load-bearing break: the mechanism that survived is not obviously the mechanism the CTO thesis needs.

### Claim K (frame) — the axis stack itself / the "compound."
**Counter: GLANCING-to-WOUNDING.** Eleven-plus axes that "stack in causal order" with an "ambient condition" meta-axis is an unfalsifiable system: with this many interacting variables and post-hoc causal ordering, *any* outcome (brake fires / brake doesn't) can be narrated. The corpus never states what observation would falsify the *compound* (as opposed to individual axes). Axis 0 (fictional media) is explicitly "hardest to operationalize," the causal claim is disclaimed down to "normalizes," and it still sits at the causal root of the compound diagram — a load-bearing position for a non-operationalized axis. The compound's persuasiveness comes from convergence, but convergence of eleven under-determined axes is weaker than it reads.

## CROSS-CUTTING METHODOLOGICAL ATTACKS

1. **The "3-0" notation systematically overstates confidence.** It denotes an internal verifier vote, not three independent sources, and not source *quality*. A "3-0 CONFIRMED" finding resting on one Wellfound page (2.1) and a "3-0" resting on peer-reviewed Bedeian & Wren (4.1b) are presented with identical notation. A reader cannot distinguish them without reading the source-class line. The Axis summary, which most downstream synthesis will lean on, propagates the confident "CONFIRMED" language while shedding the source-class caveats.

2. **Asymmetric skepticism.** The corpus applies broken-citation-chain skepticism ruthlessly to demote Axis 10 (3.10) but does **not** apply the same test to the Fayol lineage (4.1b, transmission unverified) or to the "visible attributable harm" coronation (3.9, untested). Skepticism is deployed where it demotes a claim the builder was ready to lose, and withheld where it would damage a claim the builder wants to keep.

3. **Source-class ceiling on the entire T2 empirical spine.** 2.1 — the behavioral evidence that T2 stands or falls on — has *zero* peer-reviewed sources by admission. The historical tracks (4.x) are well-sourced; the *contemporary expectation* tracks (2.x) are not. So the part of the argument that is actually about the present-day CTO is the worst-sourced part.

4. **No negative controls anywhere.** No role the frame predicts is non-composite is tested blind. No field where the brake *should* be absent but is present is sought. The corpus tests its hypotheses against confirming and self-flagged-disconfirming cases, but never against a designed negative control.

## VERDICT — Which claims are most exposed

The most exposed claims, in order: **(1) CTO-specificity (Claim C)** is effectively unsupported, not merely "under-powered" — it rests on a single comparator and the live alternative (all C-suite is composite) is unexcluded; the "under-powered" label launders absent evidence into weak evidence. **(2) The "visible attributable harm" master variable (Claim E / Lead 2)** repeats the exact single-master-variable over-reach the corpus congratulates itself for retiring, is admittedly untested, and is contradicted by ≥3 of the corpus's own brake cases and by its foundational Abbott citation. **(3) The T2 clean-separation rule (Claim A / Lead 1)** is not operationalized: role-design impossibility is re-imported under an "expectation" label, and the corpus's own stage-modulation finding points to a legibility explanation that would dissolve the framing. **(4) The Builder/Multiplier/Strategist frame (Lead 3)** is an imposed, externally-unvalidated construct used as its own coding scheme, making 2.1's "composite confirmed" findings partly circular. **(5) The dissenter-as-robust-lever (Claim I)** inverts the corpus's own evidence ordering — leading with unverified canon, hedging the verified leg — and invokes identity-belief stickiness selectively. The corpus's genuine strengths — 3.10's honest amnesia/akrasia reclassification, 4.8's CCB re-founding, 5.6's diagnostic/intervention split, and the disconfirmation discipline generally — are real and survive; but they survive partly *because* they were allowed to weaken claims, whereas the surviving load-bearing claims that the builder wants to keep (CTO-specificity, the new master variable, the clean separation) are precisely the ones that did not get the same adversarial pressure. The pattern of exposure is not random: **the corpus is most rigorous exactly where rigor demotes a claim it was willing to lose, and least rigorous exactly where rigor would damage the claims it most wants to keep.**
