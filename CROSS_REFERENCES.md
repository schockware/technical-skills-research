## ¬ß1 ‚Äî The single most important fact: it's ONE argument at three scales

The three areas are not three findings ‚Äî they are **one structural claim observed at three altitudes.** State it once, cite it everywhere:

- **Industry (widest):** software built identity-rotation into the *default* advancement path at every rung, labeled it as deepening, and withheld the cushions other rotating professions provide. *(Software Industry `R3_STEELMAN_strongest_form.md`, one-paragraph version.)*
- **Developer (career ladder):** every promotion Junior‚Üí‚Ä¶‚ÜíVP is a rotation, not a deepening; the fracture points are IC‚ÜíEM and Senior‚ÜíStaff. *(Software Developer `09_SYNTHESIS_dev_ladder_thesis.md`.)*
- **CTO (sharpest):** the same rotation, all three modes, pre-scale, alone, compressed to 18‚Äì24 months. *(CTO `08_DRAFT_skills_divergence_thesis.md`.)*

**The synthesis rule:** the CTO is the *terminal expression*, the developer ladder is the *mechanism's origin*, the industry is the *structural cause*. Each area's synthesis should state which altitude it occupies and link the other two ‚Äî not re-argue the whole stack.

- **Industry form:** "startups can't *afford* specialists" concedes the capacity ceiling is real and merely unfunded. *(`R3_STEELMAN`, "steelman of the strongest objection.")*
- **CTO form:** scope-vs-kind keystone (`AX-SCOPEKIND`) + the ~4‚Äì6:1 economic compression (`AX-COMPRESSION`) ‚Äî the industry distributes the composite the moment it can afford to, proving it never believed one person should hold it.

**These should reference each other explicitly.** It is one argument at two scales: *any economic justification for the composite is simultaneously an admission that the composite exceeds one person's capacity.* Whichever synthesis states it should note the other scale, not re-derive.
