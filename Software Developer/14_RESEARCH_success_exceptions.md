## Scope at this tier

The ICs who made hard ladder transitions **durably** — the ~50% who did *not* revert at IC→EM, and those who crossed Senior→Staff (a known plateau). The honest question is not "what did they do" but:

- For each apparent success factor: **who did the same thing and reverted anyway?** (the invisible failure cell — and at this tier it is *large and well-documented*, unlike the famous-founder cases — the ~50% reversion population is real and studied, which makes this tier's success claims *more* checkable than the CTO tier's.)
- How much "success" here is mechanism vs. company context (good manager-of-managers above them, psychological safety, a role that fit) — i.e. structural support, not individual heroics.

**The likely finding (hypothesis, pre-research):** the durable IC→EM transitions correlate with *received support* (Van Lancker's psych-safety + value-fit conditions, `../CTO/AX-VL2023`) far more than with individual traits. If so, that's the anti-recipe: the success factor isn't something the *individual* did — it's something the *org* provided. Which loops straight back to the framework's thesis.
