# Research Classification Table
## All research threads mapped to CTO Operating Modes taxonomy

**Methodology:** Each research thread classified against Builder / Multiplier / Strategist modes, with evidence strength and classification notes. Produced during session as part of "applying the taxonomy to existing research" exercise.

**Key finding:** Of ~35 threads, the overwhelming majority map to Multiplier mode. Builder mode has almost no empirical research. Strategist mode has none. This is not a taxonomy failure — it's a research population limitation that the taxonomy explains: management research studies managers of existing teams, not people doing three jobs simultaneously before any team exists.

---

## Classification key

| Badge | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Builder | Maps exclusively to CTO-Builder mode |
| Multiplier | Maps exclusively to CTO-Multiplier mode |
| B→M split | Spans Builder and Multiplier modes |
| M→S split | Spans Multiplier and Strategist modes |
| All modes | Present across all modes as threshold, not differentiator |
| Gap | No research found — confirmed gap |

## Cluster 1: Manager quality & team outcomes

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Project Oxygen (2008, updated 2018) | **Multiplier** | Causal | 10 behaviors all Multiplier. Technical skill ranked last. Zero Builder behaviors. Sample was ~2,000+ person company — no Builders in scope. Designed to prove managers don't matter. Proved the opposite. |
| Wiseman Multipliers (2010) | **Multiplier** | Causal | 150 executives, 35 companies. 2x output gap Multiplier vs Diminisher. Directly names the failure mode of Builder→Multiplier transition. Core theoretical grounding for the mode name. |
| Google Project Aristotle (2012-2014) | **Multiplier** | Causal | 180 teams. Psychological safety #1 predictor. Studied teams at scale — not pre-team Builder context. |

## Cluster 2: EQ vs TQ in technical leadership

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goleman EQ research (1998 HBR) | **Multiplier** | Strong | IQ and technical skill are thresholds. EQ competencies differentiate. Applies at and beyond Builder→Multiplier transition. |
| TQ vs EQ comparative (Folkman/Zenger) | **Multiplier** | Strong | High-EQ, low-TQ leaders outperform high-TQ, low-EQ even in technical organizations. Directly contradicts assumption that technical depth is primary CTO differentiator. |
| Accidental CTO / Gartner manager gap | **Multiplier** | Practitioner | ~1/3 tech executives self-report gaps in people development. "Accidental manager" pattern. Multiplier failure mode by another name. |
| EQ accounts for 70% of team engagement variation | **Multiplier** | Moderate | 155,000 leaders studied. Only 22% have strong EQ. Sample not startup-specific. |
| Learning agility predicts leadership advancement | **M→S split** | Strong | Adaptability predicts advancement better than domain expertise. Most relevant at mode transitions. Not mode-exclusive. |

## Cluster 3: Domain expert failure modes (TEE)

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transitive Expert Error — Mars (2026) | **Multiplier** | Theoretical | Domain expertise becomes cognitive liability at domain boundaries. Primary mechanism for Builder→Multiplier failure. Fires when Builder tries to apply engineering heuristics to people management. |
| Competence trap (organizational learning) | **Multiplier** | Strong | Over-exploitation of existing competencies, under-exploration of new ones. Maps to Builder doubling down on technical contributions when people management is required. |
| Dunning-Kruger in cross-domain leadership | **Multiplier** | Strong | Expert in one domain overestimates competence in adjacent domain. Explains why Builder→Multiplier failure is confident rather than anxious. |
| Pseudo-delegation / control retention | **Multiplier** | Moderate | Leaders formally delegate but retain de facto control. Behavioral mechanism of Diminisher pattern. |
| Structural dissent as inoculation | **Multiplier** | Strong | Designated dissenters partially inoculate against TEE. Requires someone outside CTO's domain alignment. |
| Generalist vs specialist CTO outcomes | **B→M split** | Weak | Generalists may transition better but weaker in Builder mode. Evidence thin — mostly practitioner. No empirical comparison. |

## Cluster 4: Culture fit hiring bias & homogeneity

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Culture fit as affinity bias | **Multiplier** | Strong | Homogeneous teams produce groupthink. CTO-Builder who hires in own image installs Multiplier-mode liability. Damage compounds: each hire hires similarly. |
| Culture add vs culture fit | **Multiplier** | Strong | Culture-add hiring outperforms on innovation. Relevant to CTO-Multiplier installing hiring infrastructure. |
| Domain expert alignment trap (original observation) | **Multiplier** | Theoretical | CTO's domain expertise propagates into team homogeneity. Team's agreement feels like alignment but is intellectual conformity. Original contribution to existing research. |
| Echo chamber in software teams | **Multiplier** | Moderate | Homogeneous technical teams self-reinforce dominant architectural assumptions. Cross-domain application from social media literature has limitations. |

## Cluster 5: Founder burnout & micromanagement

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founder burnout statistics | **Multiplier** | Strong | >50% of founders experienced burnout. Peaks at transition points correlating with mode boundary timing. Burnout is a symptom of Builder in Multiplier-mode conditions. |
| Micromanagement productivity costs | **Multiplier** | Strong | 74% more stress, 50% less output, 2.6x more likely to job-hunt. Quantifies cost of Builder behavior in Multiplier territory. |
| Identity threat at leadership transitions | **Multiplier** | Strong | Founders resist delegation because it challenges identity investment. Explains why Builder→Multiplier fails even when CTO intellectually understands it's needed. |
| Feedback addiction / tactical work reward | **Builder** | Strong | Immediate feedback loops of hands-on work are neurologically rewarding. Delegation offers slower satisfaction. **First finding that maps exclusively to Builder-mode psychology.** |

## Cluster 6: Premature scaling & startup failure

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Startup Genome premature scaling report | **B→M split** | Strong | 74% of startups fail from premature scaling. Maps to both modes: Builder adding Multiplier-mode process too early, and Builder delaying Multiplier mode too long after PMF. |
| Technical debt accumulation and consequences | **Builder** | Strong | Early architectural decisions persist and constrain later scaling. Technical debt consumes 20-40% of engineering capacity over time (Deloitte). Primary Builder-mode impact vector. |
| Early architecture choices as load-bearing constraints | **Builder** | Practitioner | Stack, data model, infrastructure decisions made pre-seed shape what's possible at Series A. No empirical measurement of which decisions predict outcomes. **Core Builder-mode gap.** |

## Cluster 7: Org design — CTO/VP Eng split

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| CTO vs VP Eng boundary definition | **Multiplier** | Practitioner | Hard boundary is the primary structural intervention for Multiplier-mode CTOs. Blurry splits distribute dysfunction. |

## Cluster 10: Builder mode — confirmed gaps

| Research | Mode | Evidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Which early technical decisions predict Series A success | **Gap — Builder** | None found | Practitioner consensus that early architecture matters. No empirical study measuring which specific decisions predict downstream scaling success. **Core Builder-mode empirical gap.** |
| CTO-Builder psychology: intrinsic reward of direct work | **Builder (partial)** | Adjacent | Feedback addiction finding touches this but not CTO-specific. Motivational profile shift across modes unmapped. |
| First-hire quality impact on team outcomes at seed | **Gap — Builder** | None found | Nothing measuring specific impact of CTO-Builder's first 3-5 technical hires on Multiplier-mode outcomes. The alignment trap's most testable hypothesis. |
| CTO-Strategist success and failure modes | **Gap — Strategist** | Practitioner only | First Round CTO Summit accounts exist. No peer-reviewed research. Entirely unmeasured empirically. |

**The finding:** Project Oxygen has zero Builder-mode behaviors. The entire management research literature studied populations with no Builders. The Builder mode is structurally invisible to the field because management research studies managers — and Builders aren't managing yet.
