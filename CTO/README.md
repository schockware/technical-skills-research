# CTO Operating Modes — Corpus Index

**The framework:** the CTO title contains three structurally distinct operating modes (Builder / Multiplier / Strategist) that demand skill sets diverging in *kind*, not degree. Asking one person to transform across all three — sequentially, pre-scale, unsupported — is an irrational composite, and the failure that results is structural, not personal.

**Status:** Active research. The public draft is **`DRAFT_SYNTHESIS.md`**, built on the independent adversarial review's defensible form (`R3_STEELMAN`). The skills-first reframe (`08_DRAFT`) and the original failure-first paper (`05_FULLTEXT`) are **prior framings, superseded as build targets** but kept as source-of-record. Citations under review (`07_APPENDIX`).

**Last consolidated:** 2026-06-24

## Status, scope & known limitations — read this first

This is **active research, published in the open while still under verification.** That is deliberate, and the seams are marked rather than hidden. Here is the honest boundary between what is solid and what is still provisional, so you can weigh the argument accordingly.

**What the argument rests on (solid):**
- The **skill-divergence observation** (`08_DRAFT` §1–2) and the **scope-vs-kind distinction** (`08_DRAFT` §3.2) are *logical* claims, established by inspection and a method-of-difference argument. They do not depend on contested statistics — they survive even if every supporting number below were struck.
- The **transformation model** (offload-and-acquire) is grounded in **two peer-reviewed sources read in full**: Mathias & Williams (2018) and Van Lancker et al. (2023), both *Journal of Business Venturing*. These are the only peer-reviewed anchors in the corpus, and both are verified full-text (`07_APPENDIX` `AX-MW2018`, `AX-VL2023`).

**What is still under verification (provisional):**
- Roughly **19 supporting citations remain unverified** — mostly secondary statistics (the ~4–6:1 cash-compression ratio, the Carta/Crunchbase attrition points, several C-suite origin dates, the dot-com IPO figures). Each is logged, rated for load-bearing weight, and tracked to a primary source in **[`07_APPENDIX_citation_review.md`](07_APPENDIX_citation_review.md)** — the live self-critique for this corpus. Treat any unverified statistic as illustrative until that log clears it.

**Two framing limits I hold myself to (stated in my own words, in the appendix):**
- **Investor-signaling claim** (`08_DRAFT` §3.3): this is a *causal narrative assembled from signaling evidence, not a measured causal study.* The defensible claim is that the evidence is **consistent with** the CTO title functioning as an investor signal — **not** that investors *caused* the failure. (`07_APPENDIX` `AX-INVESTOR`, `RISK-INVESTOR-CAUSAL`.)
- **The "0 Strategist findings" gap:** the research population can't yet empirically test the third (Series B) transformation. That is a **scope limit, not confirming evidence**, and is never used as support. (`07_APPENDIX` review note #3, `RISK-ASYMMETRY`.)

**Per-area self-critique:** each research area's appendix calls out its own specific weaknesses. The CTO area's is `07_APPENDIX`; it is the publish-gate, and it is public on purpose.

## The argument in one pass

1. **Observation** (`08_DRAFT` §1): a CTO's skill focus moves product → people → market. Not a ladder; a *transformation* (offload old + acquire new, in tension). Fails three ways: failure-to-acquire, burnout, slippage.
2. **Divergence is real** (`08_DRAFT` §2): industry split these skills into two tracks (IC/management) and, at scale, into separate roles. It already declared them incompatible.
3. **Irrational condition** (`08_DRAFT` §3): we demand the composite anyway, pre-scale, unsupported. §3.1 etymology (why unnamed) → §3.2 **scope-vs-kind keystone** (why only the CTO is catastrophic) → §3.3 investor signaling (who installed it).
4. **Predicted failure** (`08_DRAFT` §4): failure clusters at the transformations, not within modes. Confirmed by Greiner, Carta/Crunchbase, Kruze.
5. **Why it persists** (`10_RESEARCH`): ~4–6:1 cash compression makes it optimal for everyone but the CTO. Revealed-preference chain.
6. **Why nothing fixes it** (`12_RESEARCH`): 6 solutions, only 3 root-cause, all need the taxonomy first.
7. **Training doesn't save it** (`11_RESEARCH`): the IC→EM transition reverts ~50% even *with* support; the CTO does it alone in 1/8th the time.

## Broader context — the CTO is the flagship case, not the whole problem

Parallel research (June 2026) established that the irrational-composite pattern is **not unique to the CTO** — it runs the entire software career ladder and reflects an industry-wide failure to specialize. The CTO area remains **THE flagship paper / case study** (most developed, most vivid, most publishable); these are *supporting* material that broadens it. Scope tiers, widest → sharpest:

- **Industry level:** `../Software Industry/13_RESEARCH_software_industry_structural.md` — software constructs roles exceeding human capacity at every level; specialization is the unapplied solution.
- **Career-ladder level:** `../Software Developer/01_RESEARCH_software_developer_career.md` — every promotion is a skill *rotation* (Junior→Mid→Senior→Staff→EM→VP); the mismatch starts at the first promotion.
- **CTO level (here):** the pattern concentrated into one role at the worst moment. The acute, terminal expression.

Shared evidence already in this corpus that these lean on: the ~50% IC→EM reversion (`AX-REVERSION`), curse of expertise (`AX-CURSE`), Larson archetypes, the transformation model (`AX-MW2018`). Infra decision (2026-06-24): **CTO keeps its full appendix/conventions; the two new areas stay lightweight research notes for now** — cross-area references use explicit `../Area/` relative paths, not the shared anchor namespace.
