## Premise 1 — The Component-Equivalence Claim

> **A CTO's work in each mode is task-equivalent to a specific, separately-studied role. We assert this equivalence deliberately, and the borrowed evidence is evidence about those component roles — which the CTO provably occupies — not about an unrelated population.**

### The mapping (stated, so it can be challenged)

| CTO mode | Is task-equivalent to | Therefore evidence about… |
|---|---|---|
| **Builder** | Senior / Staff IC engineer doing hands-on architecture + build | …IC craft, IC→EM identity investment |
| **Multiplier** | Engineering Manager → VP Engineering (people, delegation, org infra) | …the IC→EM→VP transition (incl. the ~50% reversion) |
| **Strategist** | Executive / founder doing board, vision, external (the role-transition literature) | …founder role transitions (Mathias & Williams, Van Lancker) |

### Why this licenses the borrowed evidence (the answer to R1's hardest attack)

R1's strongest hit: *"the peer-reviewed anchors study founders/ENG-managers, not CTOs — using a different population's evidence to carry a CTO-specific structure."* The response is not to find CTO-specific data (it barely exists — see below). It is to state plainly:

- **The CTO-Builder *is* doing the IC/Staff engineer's tasks** — at a startup there is often literally no one else doing them. So IC→EM reversion data is not "borrowed from a different population"; it is **evidence about the exact transition the CTO-Builder must make**, because the CTO-Builder *is* an IC making the EM move (plus more, simultaneously).
- The composite is precisely **IC→EM + founder-role-transition + cross-domain expertise, combined and compressed.** The adjacent literatures aren't arbitrary substitutes — they are the **literal decomposed components** of the role.

### Why CTO-specific research doesn't exist (and why that's the field's omission, not ours)

The industry **does not make the mode distinction at all** — it studies "engineering managers," "founders," "staff engineers" as separate populations and never names the CTO as their *composite*. So:
- There is no body of "CTO mode-transition" research to borrow from, because the field never carved the role into modes to study.
- The corpus's contribution is *making that decomposition explicit* — which is exactly why it must lean on the component literatures. **The borrowing is the method, not a shortcut.**

### The honest boundary (what this premise does NOT license)

- It does **not** claim the *compression* (all three at once, pre-scale, 18–24 months) is measured. The components are evidenced; the compression is argued. (See `R3_STEELMAN`; this is the framework-vs-finding line.)
- It does **not** claim founders and CTOs are identical — founders are the better-*buffered* case (the CEO-analog with support infrastructure). The CTO is the component transitions **minus** the buffers. So borrowed founder-success mechanisms describe the *mechanism*; the CTO's *severity* is the corpus's extension. **State this at every load-bearing use.**

> **Citation rule:** anywhere the corpus uses IC→EM, founder-transition, or cross-domain evidence to make a CTO claim, cite `17_PREMISES` P1 and add the one-line boundary: *"evidence about the component role; the CTO-specific compression/severity is this corpus's extension."*

## Premise 2 — The Etymology Is Contingent, the Structure Is Not

> **The structural thesis (the skill sets diverge in kind; the composite exceeds one person) does not depend on the etymology being correct. The mis-inheritance story explains *why this particular flavor* of the problem and *why it went unnamed* — it is the contingent layer, not the load-bearing one.**

### The counterfactual that proves the separation

Imagine the 1980s enterprise CTO definition (Strategist-from-day-one) had simply *held* through the 1990s–2000s instead of being applied to startup Builders. Then:

- We would be describing a **different paradigm** — perhaps "the technical co-founder who must *become* a CTO," with the title correctly reserved for the Strategist endpoint.
- There would be **fewer callouts to irrational *choices*** — no "the title was installed as an investor signal," less of the misnomer framing.
- But **the same underlying issue would remain**: someone still has to traverse Builder → Multiplier → Strategist skill sets that diverge in kind, pre-scale, compressed. The mismatch is in the *work*, not in the *title*.

### What this does for the thesis (it strengthens it)

- It **decouples** the structural claim (robust) from the historical-accident claim (contingent). A reviewer who rejects the etymology as a "just-so story" (R1 Attack 4) **still has to face the structural claim**, because it was never resting on the etymology.
- It tells us exactly how to *use* the etymology: as the answer to **"why was this never named?"** and **"why this specific flavor?"** — never as the answer to **"is the mismatch real?"** That question is answered by Premise 1 + the divergence argument.

> **Discipline rule:** the etymology / investor-signaling / misnomer material (`08_DRAFT` §3.1, §3.3; `09_RESEARCH`) is the **contingent layer**. No conclusion may load-bear on it. If the public draft removed every etymology sentence, the structural thesis must still stand. Test the draft against that.
